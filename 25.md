# Ch. 25 On the mystery of the idea.

There is a being, chaotic but perfect. It preceded the birth of heaven and earth.

> <div class="commentary">This means that Tao is chaotic and formless and bore everything, thus preceding heaven and earth.</div>

>> <div class="note">The reading 成 "perfected" instead of 生 "bore" is meaningless.</div>

Still, O! Empty, O! Alone it stands and does not change.

> <div class="commentary">To be still is to be without sound. To be empty is to be hollow and formless. To be alone is to be mated with nobody. To be unchanging is to keep the eternal during the changes.</div>

Everywhere it goes without being endangered.

> <div class="commentary">Tao goes everywhere; in heaven and earth there is no place which it does not enter. It is within the Yang without becoming burnt; it entrusts itself to the Yin without becoming rotten. There is nothing which it does not penetrate without endangering itself or perishing.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 徧 "it goes everywhere" v.1. 通 "it penetrates everywhere".</div>

Thereby it could become the world's mother.

> <div class="commentary">Tao nourishes and educates the spiritual atmosphere of all things like a mother nourishing her children.</div>

I do not know its name. To designate it I call it Tao.

> <div class="commentary">As I do not see the form of Tao, I do not know how to designate it properly. As I see that all things are born from Tao, I designate it by calling it Tao.</div>

Forced to devise a name for it I call it the Great One.

> <div class="commentary">I do not know its name. Constrained to name it I call it the Great One. It is high and not above, enmeshing and not outside. With everything it has patience. Therefore it is called the Great One.</div>

The Great One is called the Passing One.

> <div class="commentary">As it is great, it is not like heaven which is eternally above, nor like earth which is eternally below. Thus it is passing and without a place of eternal rest.</div>

The Passing One is called distant.

> <div class="commentary">This means: What is distant is the exhaustible within the inexhaustible. It disperses its atmosphere through heaven and earth. There is nothing which it does not penetrate.</div>

Distant means returning.

> <div class="commentary">This means: Being distant it cannot be surpassed. It is cut off, and then it returns. It exists within the body of man.</div>

Therefore Tao is great, heaven is great, earth is great, the king is also great.

> <div class="commentary">The grandeur of Tao embraces heaven and earth, and there is nothing which it does not contain. For the grandeur of heaven there is nothing which it does not cover. For the grandeur of earth there is nothing which it does not sustain. For the grandeur of the king there is nothing which he does not govern.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 制 "govern" v.1. 載 "sustain", which makes no sense.</div>

Within the world there are four great ones,

> <div class="commentary">The four great ones are Tao, heaven, earth and the king. They all possess grandeur and splendour. This is not their highest [achievement]. What is called Tao, this possesses what depends on it. If it possesses what depends on it, then it is called Tao. Then the grandeur which lies in the designation of the Tao is not like the grandeur of the undesignated one. The undesignated one cannot acquire glory. It is called the world. Heaven, earth and the king are within the undesignated one. Therefore it is said: Within the world there are the four great ones.</div>

> <div class="commentary">This part of the commentary is missing in the Tao-tsang edition.</div>

and the king dwells within their unity.

> <div class="commentary">Within the eight poles there are the four great ones. The king dwells within their unity. To dwell within unity constitutes his residence.</div>

Man should take earth for his model.

> <div class="commentary">Man ought to take earth for his model. It is peaceful and quiet, soft and harmonious. By sowing in it one obtains the five grains. By digging in it one reaches the sweet springs. It works without hatred. It has merit without dealing out punishments.</div>

Earth should take heaven for its model.

> <div class="commentary">Heaven is so still and without motion. It gives without demanding compensation. It generates and brings up all beings. It receives and takes nothing.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 湛然 "so still" v.1. | 泊 "quiet and content".</div>

Heaven should take Tao for its model.

> <div class="commentary">Tao is tranquil and quiescent and does not talk. Yin and Yang let the atmosphere of the spirit circulate. All things are spontaneously perfected.</div>

Tao takes itself for its model.

> <div class="commentary">Tao is by nature itself. There is nothing else which it could take for its model.</div>
