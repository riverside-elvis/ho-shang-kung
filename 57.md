# Ch. 57 How to simplify customs.

By attaining justice one governs a country.

> <div class="commentary">以 = 至 to attain. Heaven which wants to rectify the personality of a man allows him to come into possession of a country.</div>

>> <div class="note">The Tao-tsang edition reads 洽 "governs", like Wang Pi's text, whereas Li Ch'iao's text has 理 "regulates".</div>

By attaining deceit one uses weapons.

> <div class="commentary">奇 = 詐 to deceive. Heaven, if wanting to deceive a man, allows him to use weapons.</div>

>> <div class="note">As this sentence and the next one are parallels to the foregoing one, Ho-shang-kung evidently wants 以 to be taken in the same sense he gives to it there, though he does not expressly say so.</div>

By attaining inactivity one takes the empire.

> <div class="commentary">By inactivity and non-action a man may take the empire and make himself its master.</div>

How do I know that this is so? By this.

> <div class="commentary">This is the presence. Lao-tse says: How do I know the intentions of heaven to be such? By what I see today I know them.</div>

>> <div class="note">Compare the last notes on ch. 21.</div>

The more prohibitions there are in the empire, the poorer the people are.

> <div class="commentary">By the empire the prince is meant. 忌誖 chi-hui are prohibitions. If the law becomes oppressive, malignity arises. If there are many prohibitions, the subjects become deceitful. They all deceive each other. Therefore they will become poor.</div>

>> <div class="note">The Tao-tsang edition commences ch. 57 with this sentence.</div>

The more sharp tools the people have, the more are state and family thrown into disorder.

> <div class="commentary">The sharp tools are power. When the people have much power, then the eyes of those who see are blinded, the ears of those who hear are confused. Prince and subjects are not attached to each other. Therefore state and family are confused.</div>

The more adroitness and intelligence man has, the more curious things appear.

> <div class="commentary">Man is the prince. Polymathy and adroitness, plastics and pictures, palaces, carvings and multicoloured garments are curious things. The more they appear, the more the subjects turn against the prince. Ornamental gold and carved gems, ornamented silks, variegated colours daily more appear.</div>

>> <div class="note">The characters "palaces, carvings and multicoloured garments" are missing in one edition, as well as the words "variegated colours".</div>

The more laws are published, the more robbers are there.

> <div class="commentary">The law is something good. The more beautiful and good things become known, the more the affairs of agriculture are neglected, and hunger and cold penetrate everywhere. Therefore there are many robbers and thieves.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 法好也 "the law is something good" v.1. 法物好物也 "the law is a good thing".</div>

Therefore a saint says: I do nothing, and the people change of themselves.

> <div class="commentary">Therefore a saint speaks as follows: I do not act, and the people change of themselves. A saint says: I cultivate Tao and receive [the commands of] heaven. I do not need to change anything, and the people become perfect of themselves.</div>

I am without activity, and the people become rich of themselves.

> <div class="commentary">I do not call the people to service but let them stay with their business. Therefore everybody becomes rich of himself.</div>

I love quietude, and the people are law-abiding of themselves.

> <div class="commentary">The saint says: If I love quietude, if I neither speak nor act, then all the people will be loyal and law-abiding of themselves.</div>

I am without desires, and the people are simple of themselves.

> <div class="commentary">If I am always without desires, if I do away with externals, then the people will follow me and remain simple and natural.</div>

>> <div class="note">One edition adds after "if I do away with externals": "if I hold garments and ornaments to be of little value".</div>

I am without feelings, and the people are pure.

> <div class="commentary">The saint says: If I practise Tao and sincerity and do away with the six feelings, the people will follow me and be pure.</div>

>> <div class="note">This part of the commentary, together with the sentence it explains, is only contained in the Tao-tsang edition.</div>
