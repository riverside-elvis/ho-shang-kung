# Ch. 32 On the Te of the saint.

Tao is eternally without a name.

> <div class="commentary">Tao may be Yin or Yang, may grow or decline, may exist or perish. Therefore it is without a constant name.</div>

>> <div class="note">The last explanation, 無常名, is rather curious, as the text has 常無名. Probably Ho-shang-kung's text had originally the same wording as the commentary, as is also supposed by Ma Hsü-lun, Lao-tse ho-ku I, 137b.</div>

It is simple though small. In the empire nobody dares to serve it.

> <div class="commentary">Tao is simple though small, subtle, mysterious and shapeless. Within the empire nobody risks to be a servant serving Tao.</div>

If the princes and the king were able to serve it, everything would spontaneously submit.

> <div class="commentary">If the princes and the king were able to retain Tao and non-action, everything would spontaneously submit and follow the changes of Te.</div>

Heaven and earth would unite to send down the sweet dew.

> <div class="commentary">If the princes and the king are able to adapt themselves in their doings to the changes of heaven and earth, then heaven sends down sweet dew as a good omen.</div>

As to the people, if nobody orders them about, they are of themselves equal.

> <div class="commentary">If heaven sends down favourable omina, then the creatures have nobody to govern them and become of themselves equal as if they were unified.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 嘉 "favourable" v.1. 善 "good".</div>

The beginning rules those having names.

> <div class="commentary">The beginning is Tao. Those having names are the creatures. Tao, as having no name, is able to govern those that have names. What is shapeless entirely rules what is shaped.</div>

Names also exhaust existence.

> <div class="commentary">既 = 盡 to exhaust. The things, as they have names, are exhausted through their existence. Their essence strives to rebel against Tao and to become separated from Te. Thereby their bodies are ruined and defiled.</div>

Heaven will also know when to stop.

> <div class="commentary">If man is able to take Tao for his model and to follow Te, heaven will also of itself know when to stop.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 法 "model" there is the senseless reading 譌 "wrongly". Instead of 知止 "to know when to stop" v.1. 知之 "know it", which conforms to a similar variant of Lao-tse's text.</div>

If it knows when to stop, then there will be no danger.

> <div class="commentary">If heaven knows when to stop, then the magical power of the spirits is preserved and not endangered.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 天 "heaven" v.1. 夫 "now if one knows when to stop". Instead of 止 "to stop" v.1. 之 "by knowing this", which again follows a different reading of the text.</div>

Tao's existence in the world may be compared to the relations of the rivers and torrents to the [Yang-tse-]chiang and the sea.

> <div class="commentary">This means: Tao's relations within the world to man correspond to the harmony with which rivers and torrents mutually mingle their floods with the [Yang-tse-]chiang and the sea.</div>
