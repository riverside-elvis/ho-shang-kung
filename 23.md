# Ch. 23 How to empty nothingness.

To speak little is natural.

> <div class="commentary">To speak little means to save words. To save words is the natural way.</div>

A whirlwind does not outlast a morning. A shower does not outlast a day.

> <div class="commentary">A whirlwind is a sudden wind. A shower is a violent rain. This means that suddenness is not able to last and violence not able to endure.</div>

Who effects this? Heaven and earth.

> <div class="commentary">Heaven and earth in their highest spiritual union produce whirlwinds and showers, but they are not yet able to let them outlast a morning. How much less ought one to have desires, when violence is so transient!</div>

Thus who in his actions follows Tao

> <div class="commentary">To follow means to do. In doing business one ought to be like Tao, peaceful and quiet, not like a whirlwind or a shower.</div>

>> <div class="note">The explanation "to follow means to do" is missing in the Che-yao.</div>

is a Taoist who becomes identical with Tao.

> <div class="commentary">By a Taoist a man who loves Tao is meant. To become identical with Tao means to identify oneself with Tao.</div>

>> <div class="note">The v.1. 爲 "is" instead of 謂 "means" is rejected by Li Ch'iao.</div>

A Teist becomes identical with Te.

> <div class="commentary">By a Teist a man who loves Te is meant. To become identical with Te means to identify oneself with Te.</div>

>> <div class="note">Li Ch'iao's reading 德 instead of | 者 is probably a mere misprint. It must doubtless be rejected, as the text as well as the wording of the preceding parallel explanation require 德者.</div>

A Sheist becomes identical with She.

> <div class="commentary">She means to give way to oneself and to lose the others. To become identical with She means to identify oneself with She.</div>

>> <div class="note">I have not translated She (失 "loss"), as Ho-shang-kung's explanation as well as Lao-tse's text itself show that in all probability She is a personification, perhaps like Tao and Te a divinity (of the pantheon of Ch'u?).</div>

Who becomes identical with Tao, Tao is also glad to get him.

> <div class="commentary">If somebody identifies himself with Tao, Tao is also glad to get him.</div>

Who becomes identical with Te, Te is also glad to get him.

> <div class="commentary">If somebody identifies himself with Te, Te is also glad to get him.</div>

Who becomes identical with She, She is also glad to get him.

> <div class="commentary">If somebody identifies himself with She, She is also glad to get him.</div>

>> <div class="note">The three foregoing explanations clearly show that Ho-shang-kung as well as Lao-tse himself here regards not only Tao but also Te and She as personal beings. In the last sentence, Li Ch'iao reads 失 "to lose" instead of 得 te "to get". In view of the parallelism of the foregoing sentences, this is probably wrong, therefore I have altered she into te. If Li Ch'iao's reading should be correct, it can of course not be understood to mean "She is glad to lose him" but must be interpreted as "She is also glad to make him a She" i.e. to identify him with itself.  
--- In the Tao-tsang edition, the explanations of the last three sentences are missing.</div>

If faith is not sufficient,

> <div class="commentary">If the prince has not sufficient faith in his subjects, then the subjects are equally without faith in their prince.</div>

>> <div class="note">The v.1. 不足 "insufficient" instead of 不信 "without faith" is rightly rejected by Li Ch'iao.</div>

then there is no faith.

> <div class="commentary">This means: Things of the same sort turn towards each other; harmonious sounds correspond to each other. Clouds follow the dragon, winds follow the tiger. Water flows to the wet, fire approaches the dry. This is the calculation of what is natural.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 數 "calculation" v.1. 類 "kind", rejected by Li Ch'iao.</div>
