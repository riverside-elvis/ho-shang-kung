# Ch. 80 How to stand alone.

The country ought to be small and the people sparse.

> <div class="commentary">Though a saint may rule a large country, he ought to treat it like a small and restricted area and not to be extravagant. Though a nation be numerous, it ought to look sparse and not conspicuous.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 寡乏 "sparse" v.1. | 少 "few".</div>

Let them be divided in tens and hundreds.

> <div class="commentary">If one lets everybody within the people have his clan and village, his divisions into tens and hundreds, then the poor and the rich, the gentry and the low-born will not turn against each other.</div>

>> <div class="note">The characters 貧富 "the poor and the rich" are wanting in some editions.</div>

As to the tools of the people, one ought not to use them.

> <div class="commentary">The tools are the tools of agriculture. One ought not to use them [means that] one ought not to call for the people and to tear them away [from agriculture] during the good season.</div>

Let the people regard death as important.

> <div class="commentary">If the ruler is able to make the people promote the useful and keep away from the hurtful, so that everybody gets his place, then the people will take death serious and hanker after life.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 貪 "hanker" v.1. 貴 "esteem".</div>

Then they will not go far away.

> <div class="commentary">If one governs without being troublesome, then the people are content with their professions. Therefore they do not go away and do not depart from their permanent habitations.</div>

Though there be ships and carriages, they will go nowhere.

> <div class="commentary">One ought to be quiet and without action, one ought not to make outward display, one ought not to love pleasures outside or within the house.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 靜 "quiet" v.1. 淨 "pure", doubtless wrong.</div>

Though one may have arms one ought not to show them.

>> <div class="note">One ought not to entertain hatred against the empire.</div>

Let the people return to the knotted strings and use them.

> <div class="commentary">Do away with externals and return to reality. Be sincere and without fraud.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 不 "not" v.1. 無, the same.</div>

Make their food sweet to them.

> <div class="commentary">Let their simple meals be agreeable to them and do not take their food away from the people.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 餐 "food" v.1. 食, the same.</div>

Make their clothes beautiful to them.

> <div class="commentary">Let their coarse garments appear beautiful to them and do not induce them to esteem variegated colours.</div>

Make their homes peaceful to them.

> <div class="commentary">Let them find peace within their reed-huts and do not instigate them to like ornamented dwellings.</div>

Make their customs agreeable to them.

> <div class="commentary">Let them be pleased with their simple customs, so that they do not want to change them.</div>

Though neighbouring countries may be mutually visible and the voices of the cocks and dogs audible,

> <div class="commentary">If their mutual distance is near.</div>

The people will reach old age and death without mutual intercourse.

> <div class="commentary">They have no wish for such.</div>
