# Ch. 73 How to act adequately.

Who is brave in daring will be killed.

> <div class="commentary">To act bravely and daring, this kills the body.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 則 "this" v.1. 卽 "then". Instead of the final particle 也 v.1. 矣.</div>

Who is brave in not-daring, will live.

> <div class="commentary">To act bravely and not daring, this keeps the body alive.</div>

>> <div class="note">In Li Ch'iao's edition, 不 "not" is missing by misprint.</div>

Of these two,

> <div class="commentary">Daring and not daring are meant.</div>

one is blissful, one is hurtful.

> <div class="commentary">To keep the body alive is blissful. To kill the body is hurtful.</div>

What heaven hates,

> <div class="commentary">It hates action.</div>

Who knows the reason of this?

> <div class="commentary">Who is able to know the reason of heaven's opinion without becoming rebellious?</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1.: "without daring to become rebellious".</div>

Therefore the saint seems to feel this as hard.

> <div class="commentary">This means: The shining Te of the saint seems to feel this brave daring as hard, bow much more the Te of one who is not a saint that strives to follow him.</div>

It is the way of heaven not to contend but to vanquish well.

> <div class="commentary">Heaven does not contend with man for things valuable or worthless, but man is afraid of it.</div>

It does not talk but is well corresponded to.

> <div class="commentary">Heaven does not talk. All things move of themselves and correspond to the seasons.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 應以時 "correspond to the seasons" v.1. 以應時 "in order to corespond to the seasons".  
---The explanation seems to allude to Lun-yü 17, 19.</div>

It does not call but they come themselves.

> <div class="commentary">Heaven does not call the things. All turn their backs to the Yin and embrace the Yang.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1.: "All the things . . .". The last sentence alludes to ch. 42.</div>

It is so vast and still plans well.

> <div class="commentary">□ = 寬 vast. It is the way of heaven, in spite of its vastness, to make good plans for timid men in their affairs, so that they may cultivate the good and hate the evil. Everybody receives his recompense.</div>

Heaven's net is very wide-meshed. It is coarse and still loses nothing.

> <div class="commentary">As regards the net of heaven, it has wide meshes and is very large. Though it is coarse and wide, it overlooks the good and bad within man. Nothing escapes it.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1.: "The net of heaven, it has. . .".</div>
