# Ch. 49 How to trust in Te.

The saint has no constant mind.

> <div class="commentary">The saint gets with difficulty accustomed to changes and esteems constancy as if he had no mind of his own.</div>

He makes the people's mind his mind.

> <div class="commentary">What is convenient to the people, on that he relies and follows it.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 從之 "follows it" 重之 "thinks it important".</div>

What is good, that I think good.

> <div class="commentary">What the people think good, the saint therefore also thinks good.</div>

What is not good, that I also think not good.

> <div class="commentary">Though there are also bad things for the saint, he changes them and causes them to become good.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1.: If there is something that is not good, he teaches it by means of Tao and induces it to become good.</div>

Te is good.

> <div class="commentary">Through the Te of the saint all the people are turned good.</div>

> <div class="commentary">v.1.: The Te of all the people is turned by the saint to goodness.</div>

The believable, I believe it.

> <div class="commentary">What all the people believe, the saint therefore believes as well.</div>

The unbelievable, I also believe it.

> <div class="commentary">What the people do not believe, the saint alters so as to make it believable.</div>

>> <div class="note">One edition adds "also by teaching them with Tao".</div>

Te is belief.

> <div class="commentary">The Te of the saint changes the people and induces them to believe.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1.: If the Te of the people changes, then the saint induces them to believe.</div>

The saint lives full of fear within the empire.

> <div class="commentary">The saint within the empire is always frightened and anxious. Though rich and honoured, he dare not be haughty and prodigal.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 怵 "frightened" v.1. 怖, same meaning.</div>

Because of the empire he obscures his mind.

> <div class="commentary">This means that because of the people within the empire the saint obscures his mind, as if he were stupid and not penetrating.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 渾 "obscures" v.1. 混, with the same meaning.</div>

All the people use their ears and eyes.

> <div class="commentary">注 = 用 to use. All the people use their ears and eyes in order to see and to hear the saint.</div>

The saint treats them all as children.

> <div class="commentary">The saint loves the people and thinks of them as of his children. He educates and nourishes them and does not expect thanks from them.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of "as of his children" v.1.: "as if he were educating children".</div>
