# Ch. 65 On the Te of simplicity.

Those who of old well practised Tao,

> <div class="commentary">By this those are meant who of old with Tao well practised asceticism and governed the country.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 謂 "are meant" v.1. 說 "are called".</div>

Thereby did not enlighten the people.

> <div class="commentary">They did not enlighten the people with Tao. Enlightenment is cunning hypocrisy.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 明知 "enlightenment" v.1. | 智, the same. Instead of 功詐 "cunning hypocrisy" v.1. 奸巧 "deceitful cunning".</div>

They wanted to keep them stupid.

> <div class="commentary">By Tao and Te they wanted to teach the people how to keep simple and plain and not to become hypocrites.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1. "plain and simple".</div>

That the people are difficult to govern is because of the abundance of their knowledge.

> <div class="commentary">As their wisdom is too much, they thus become cunning and hypocritical.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 而 "thus" v.1. 作 "this causes them to become . . .".</div>

Therefore to govern a country by wisdom is to rob the country.

> <div class="commentary">When the affairs of the government of a country are left to wise and intelligent men, it will surely secede from Tao and Te, and perversity will be its highest bliss. This is to rob the country.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 慧 "intelligent" v.1. 惠 "benign". Instead of 遠 "it will secede" v.1. 背 "it will turn its back to". Instead of 爲 "this is" v.1. 乃是 "and this is".</div>

To govern a country without wisdom is the country's bliss.

> <div class="commentary">When the affairs of a country are left to men neither wise nor intelligent, then the people keep to the right and do not work unto badness and outwardness. Ruler and subjects are near to each other, prince and minister work together. Thereby they bring the country's bliss about.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 慧 "intelligent" v.1. 惠 "benign". The first sentence is missing in the Che-yao.</div>

Who knows these two will also be a model and pattern.

> <div class="commentary">These two mean wisdom and ignorance. The wise man necessarily brings about theft and the ignorant man bliss. He himself is a model for the practice of asceticism and the government of the country.</div>

>> <div class="note">Behind 當 "necessarily" one edition adds 常能 "is always able to". Instead of 智者 "the wise man" v.1. 智 "wisdom", instead of 不知者 "the ignorant man" v.1. 不知 "ignorance". Instead 爲福 "brings about bliss" v.1. 能 | | "is able to bring about bliss".</div>

To know always the model and pattern, this is called the Te of the dark one.

> <div class="commentary">The dark one is heaven. To be able to know the pattern for the government of the country and the practice of asceticism, this means a Te which is identical with [that of] heaven.</div>

>> <div class="note">On this interpretation of hsüan "dark" see note on ch. 1.</div>

The Te of the dark one is deep and far away.

> <div class="commentary">The man who possesses the Te of the dark one is so deep that he cannot be fathomed, and so far away that be cannot be reached.</div>

The contrary of things.

> <div class="commentary">The man who possesses the Te of the dark one is the contrary of all beings. All beings wish to complete themselves, [but he who possesses] the Te of the dark one wishes to promote others.</div>

And then he reaches the great obedience.

>> <div class="note">Li Ch'iao notes that the last sentence is quoted in the commentary of Hsi Shu-ye's 嵇叔衣 (Hsi K'ang's 嵇康) Yang-sheng-lun 養生論 (Wen-hsüan 53,4b) as follows: 河上公曰大順者天理也 "Ho-shang-kung says: The great obedience is the principle of heaven".</div>
