# Ch. 42 On the changes of Tao.

Tao generates one. One generates two. Two generate three. Three generate all things.

> <div class="commentary">Tao generates one: what Tao generates in the beginning, is one. One generates two: it generates Yin and Yang. Two generate three: Yin and Yang generate the harmonious, the clear and the turbid, the three atmospheres which are divided into heaven, earth and man. Three generate all beings: heaven, earth and man together generate everything. Heaven expands, earth changes, man educates and nourishes.</div>

All things turn away from Yin and embrace Yang.

> <div class="commentary">Among all things there is none which did not turn away from Yin and embrace Yang. They improve on the same day.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 抱, "embrace" v.1. 向 "turn towards",</div>

The empty breath effects union.

> <div class="commentary">In all things there is the original breath. Thereby they reach their union with weakness, as within the breast there are the intestines, within the bones there is the marrow, within plants there is emptiness. The harmonious breath secretly pervades them. Thereby they obtain long life.</div>

What is odious to men, that are orphans, widows and "naveless ones". But king and dukes take these for their designations.

> <div class="commentary">Orphan, widow and "naveless one" are unlucky names, but the princes and the king take them for their designations. Remain in humility, take emptiness for your model and become united to weakness.</div>

>> <div class="note">On the title pu-ku "naveless one" see note on ch. 39.</div>

Thereby some of the creatures lose it and win,

> <div class="commentary">By attracting it, it is not won. If repelled, it is sure to return.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 引 "to attract" v.1. 取 "to seize". Instead of 推之 "if repelled" v.1. | 讓 "if declined". One edition adds a quotation from Wen-tse, doubtless a later addition, as Ho-shang-kung never quotes another author by name.</div>

win it and lose.

> <div class="commentary">Who exalts himself, breaks down. Who strives for riches, gets into misfortune.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 致 "gets into" v.1. 得 "attains".</div>

What others teach,

> <div class="commentary">This means the teachings of others with regard to the flight from weakness and the practice of strength, the flight from tenderness and the practice of hardness.</div>

I teach it too.

> <div class="commentary">He says: I teach men to flee from strength and to practise weakness, to flee from hardness and to practise tenderness.</div>

The violent ones do not reach their [natural] death.

> <div class="commentary">The violent ones are those who do not believe in the mystery and rebel against Tao and Te. They do not follow the classical doctrines, honour power and use violence. They do not reach their [natural] death, because heaven cuts off [their life]. Weapons attack them, the king's law kills them. They do not reach the [natural] death of their life.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1.: "they do not reach the end of their life but die". Ching-chiao is perhaps not to be understood in its general meaning "doctrine of the classics" but as "doctrine of the Ching" i.e. the Tao-te-ching, such as Han Fei always calls the Tao-te-ching 書 shu "the Book", a designation otherwhere only applied to the Shu-ching.</div>

I want to make these the fathers of my doctrine.

> <div class="commentary">The father is the beginning. Lao-tse makes of the men of violence the beginning of the commandments of his doctrine.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 戒 "commandments" v.1. 式 "model", rejected by Li Ch'iao.</div>
