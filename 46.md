# Ch. 46 How to moderate desires.

When there is Tao within the empire,

> <div class="commentary">This means when the prince possesses Tao.</div>

then the racing horses are used for manuring.

> <div class="commentary">To manure means to manure the fields. When arms are not used, then the racing horses are used for to work in the fields. By practising asceticism one uses the Yang breath for manuring the body.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 糞田 "to manure the fields" v.1. 治 | "to cultivate the fields".</div>

If there is no Tao within the empire,

> <div class="commentary">This means, if the prince possesses no Tao.</div>

war horses are reared in the suburbs.

> <div class="commentary">When war is not stopped, war horses are bred within the boundaries of the suburbs and do not return for a long time.</div>

Of sins there is none greater than to be able to desire.

> <div class="commentary">To love sensuality.</div>

Of misfortunes there is none greater than to know no contentedness.

> <div class="commentary">Wealth and honour cannot be spontaneously forbidden.</div>

Of defects none is greater than striving for gain.

> <div class="commentary">If one wants to obtain men or things, reason becomes avidity.</div>

Therefore, if one knows the contentedness of contentedness,

> <div class="commentary">If one preserves one's root.</div>

this is eternal contentedness.

> <div class="commentary">A heart without desires.</div>

>> <div class="note">The character 心 "heart" is missing in one edition.</div>
