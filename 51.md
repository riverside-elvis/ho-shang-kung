# Ch. 51 How to nourish Te.

Tao generates them.

> <div class="commentary">Tao generates all beings.</div>

Te nourishes them.

> <div class="commentary">Te is unity. Unity generates and spreads the breath and nourishes it.</div>

It forms them into things and shapes.

> <div class="commentary">Unity creates all things and forms their shapes.</div>

By power it completes them.

> <div class="commentary">Unity creates all things and effects the power of the breath of cold and heat. Thereby it completes them.</div>

>> <div class="note">In the Tao-tsang edition 炁, "breath" is wanting.</div>

Therefore there are none among all beings that did not honour Tao and esteem Te.

> <div class="commentary">What Tao and Te effect is entirely perfect. If you are unquiet, you should honour them.</div>

Tao is honoured and Te esteemed. Now they command nobody but remain always natural.

> <div class="commentary">Tao is the One which does not command. Though it calls forth all creatures, it always remains natural. They correspond to it like the shadow and the echo.</div>

Therefore Tao generates them, nourishes them, makes them grow, educates them, perfects them, warms them, feeds them, protects them.

> <div class="commentary">Tao's relation to things not only consists in its begetting them, as if that were enough. It causes them to grow, nourishes, perfects, warms, protects and educates them. It induces everything to become perfect according to its own nature. A prince who governs a country and practises asceticism ought to resemble it.</div>

It generates without possessing.

> <div class="commentary">Tao generates all things but keeps none of them to make profit.</div>

It acts without making claims.

> <div class="commentary">If Tao completes something, it does not look out for a requital.</div>

It educates without governing.

> <div class="commentary">Though Tao educates and nourishes everything, it does not govern [things] in order to put them to use.</div>

This is called mysterious Te.

> <div class="commentary">Benevolent Te which Tao sets in motion is mysterious. It is impossible to behold.</div>
