# Ch. 70 How to know difficulties.

The words of the ego are very easy to know and very easy to follow.

> <div class="commentary">Lao-tse knows that what the ego says is little and easy to know, simple and easy to follow.</div>

Within the empire nobody is able to know them, and nobody is able to follow them.

> <div class="commentary">Men loathe what is tender and weak and like what is hard and strong.</div>

Words have an ancestor. Deeds have a master.

> <div class="commentary">What the ego says, has an ancestor and a master. Among deeds there are princes and subjects, superiors and inferiors. Then men of the world do not know this. This is not because the ego had no Te but because the mind is opposed to the ego.</div>

Now because this is unknown, therefore the ego is unknown.

> <div class="commentary">That the men of the world do not know this is because of the obscurity and external invisibility of the Tao and Te of the ego, the mystery of its extreme infinity and subtility. Therefore they do not know this.</div>

>> <div class="note">"Tao" is wanting in one edition. Instead of 闐昧 "invisibility" v.1. 昧, which has the same meaning. A variant reads: Now it is the saint who pushes the Te of the ego in its obscurity and outward invisibility to the extreme boundaries of the subtle and the mysterious, Therefore he is unknown.</div>

Those who know the ego are few. Therefore the ego is of value.

> <div class="commentary">希 = 少 few. Only he who penetrates Tao may recognize the ego. Therefore it is of value.</div>

Therefore the saint dresses in haircloth and hides the gem within his bosom.

> <div class="commentary">Who dresses in haircloth is outwardly simple. Who hides a gem within his bosom has inward fulness. Hide your treasures, conceal your Te and do not show it to others.</div>

>> <div class="note">The Che-yao, instead of the last sentence, gives only the words 為貴也 "and is of value".</div>
