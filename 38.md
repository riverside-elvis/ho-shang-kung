# Ch. 38 Discussion on Te.

Superior Te is without Te. Therefore it possesses Te.

> <div class="commentary">By superior Te the princes of highest antiquity are meant. Their Te was great and nameless. Therefore it is called superior Te. They are called without Te, as they did not use Te to instruct the people. They were conservative and natural and nourished the life of the people. Their Te was invisible. Therefore they are called without Te. It is said that their Te became one with heaven and earth, became one with the currents of the atmosphere. Thereby the people attained perfection.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of "nameless" v.1. 無上 "without superiority". The last two sentences are missing in the Che-yao.</div>

Inferior Te does not lose Te. Therefore it is not Te.

> <div class="commentary">By inferior Te the princes are meant who have got names and designations. Their Te was not equal to the superior Te. Therefore it is called inferior Te. Who does not lose Te, his Te can be seen, his merit can be exalted. By having a name it reaches his personality. Therefore it is not Te.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 功 "merit" v.1. 言 "words". The two last sentences are equally missing in the Che-yao.</div>

Superior Te is without action, and thereby nothing is done.

> <div class="commentary">This means: Take Tao for your model and be peaceful and quiet. Have nothing to do. This is called nameless action.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 言 "this is called" v.1. 謂 "this means".</div>

Inferior Te acts and has thereby action.

> <div class="commentary">This means the business of ordering and governing. It means that one thereby creates a name for oneself.</div>

Superior humanity acts, and nothing is thereby done.

> <div class="commentary">By superior humanity a prince following humanity is meant. There is nothing superior to his humanity. Therefore it is called superior humanity. Who acts according to it, acts according to humanity and benignity. His merit is accomplished, his doings are established, and he does not strive for action.</div>

Superior justice acts and thereby has action.

> <div class="commentary">Justice is applied for making decisions. By doing so one cares for oneself. By killing men one establishes authority. One taxes the subjects in order to make presents to oneself.</div>

Superior ceremonial acts, and nobody corresponds to it.

> <div class="commentary">By this a prince of high-standing ceremonial is meant. There is nothing more elated than his ceremonial. Therefore it is called superior ceremonial. This means: To act according to the ceremonial and thus to give regulations is the ritual of hypocrisy. When the flower is displayed, the fruit perishes. Outwardness and hypocrisy are thereby multiplied. Activity detaches itself from Tao and is not able to correspond to it.</div>

Thereupon it stretches out its arms and forces them.

> <div class="commentary">This means: Who talks much cannot correspond to it. The prince and his subjects contend with each other. Therefore it stretches out its arms and draws them towards each other.</div>

>> <div class="note">Another variant of the first sentence runs: This means: The ceremonial is very troublesome, and nobody is able to correspond to it.</div>

Therefore: If one loses Tao, then Te follows.

> <div class="commentary">This means: If Tao decays, then Te originates.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1.: Tao decays and Te originates.</div>

If one loses Te, then humanity originates.

> <div class="commentary">This means: If Te decays, then humanity and love become visible.</div>

If one loses humanity, then justice originates.

> <div class="commentary">This means: If humanity decays, then one distinguishes the light of justice.</div>

If one loses humanity, then the ceremonial originates.

> <div class="commentary">This means: If justice decays, then one exhibits the ceremonial according to the presents, so as to walk about with jade and silk.</div>

Now the ceremonial is the diminution of faith and sincerity

> <div class="commentary">This means: The ceremonial neglects the fundamentals and cultivates superficiality. Therefore faith and sincerity decay from day to day.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 衰 "decay" v.1. 消 "vanish".</div>

and the head of confusion.

> <div class="commentary">The ceremonious man slights reality and esteems outwardness. Therefore justice and uprightness daily diminish, and vileness and confusion daily start up.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 少 "diminish" v.1. 消 "vanish".</div>

Foreknowledge is the flower of Tao

> <div class="commentary">If one knows nothing, then one talks. Knowledge creates foreknowledge. Such a man loses the reality of Tao and obtains its flower.</div>

and the beginning of stupidity.

> <div class="commentary">This means: A man who possesses foreknowledge leads to the beginning of stupidity and obscuration.</div>

>> <div class="note">The Tao-tsang edition reads: The stupidity of man is the beginning of obscuration.</div>

Therefore a great man dwells within its fulness.

> <div class="commentary">A great man is a prince who possesses Tao and Te. Who dwells in fulness allows the body to dwell in simplicity.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of "who possesses Tao and Te" v.1. "who has acquired Tao and Te".</div>

He does not dwell in its scantiness.

> <div class="commentary">Who does not remain with his body and resists Tao, is troubled within the world.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 煩 "troubled" v.1. 煽 "deceived".</div>

He dwells within its fruit.

> <div class="commentary">He dwells in faith and sincerity.</div>

He does not dwell within its flower.

> <div class="commentary">He lays no stress on words.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1.: 言華 "on the flower of words".</div>

Therefore he avoids that and seizes this.

> <div class="commentary">He avoids yonder outwardness and seizes this reality.</div>
