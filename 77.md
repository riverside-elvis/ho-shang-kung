# Ch. 77 On the way of heaven.

Should the way of heaven not resemble the bending of a bow?

> <div class="commentary">It is the way of heaven to be dark and profound. It chooses the different sorts of things to use them for examples.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 邈 "profound" v.1. 味 "obscure".</div>

It presses down what is high. It raises what is low. It diminishes what has too much. It enriches what has too little.

> <div class="commentary">This means: In bending a bow it is adjusted. Being so it can be used. For when the high is pressed down and the low drawn upwards, the strong diminished and the weak repleted, then this is the way of heaven.</div>

>> <div class="note">At the end of the first sentence, there is a variant containing 義: "This is the method of adjusting a bow by bending it." At the close of the second sentence, a variant has the final particle 耳.</div>

It is the way of heaven to diminish what has too much and to replete what has not enough.

> <div class="commentary">It is the way of heaven to diminish what has a surplus and to replete what is deficient. It always thinks adjustment to be superior.</div>

>> <div class="note">The words "to diminish what has a surplus and" are wanting in one edition. Instead of 爲上 "to superior" v.1. 是尙 "to be preferable".</div>

The way of man is not like this.

> <div class="commentary">The way of man is contrary to that of heaven.</div>

He diminishes what has not enough and repletes what has too much.

> <div class="commentary">The men of the world take from the poor to give to the rich. They rob the weak to benefit the strong.</div>

Who is able to have abundance to give it to the empire? Only he who has Tao.

> <div class="commentary">This means: Who is able to remain on the throne where he has got abundance and to be himself sparing with dignities and emoluments in order to give to those within the empire who have not got enough? Only a prince possessing Tao is able to act thus.</div>

Therefore the saint acts without making claims.

> <div class="commentary">The saint produces Te and displays it without claiming recompense.</div>

>> <div class="note">After 施 "displays" one reading has 惠 "benevolence", meaning thus "and displays benevolence". After 恃: "claims" one text adds 望 "longs", thus reading "without claiming recompense or longing for it".</div>

When merit has been accomplished, he does not stay.

> <div class="commentary">By his merit he accomplishes his affairs, and then he does not stay on the throne.</div>

He does not wish to show himself as a worthy.

> <div class="commentary">He does not wish to show himself to men so that they may recognize the worthiness of his self. He declines merit, does not stay within the splendour, is afraid of heaven and diminishes whatever has got too much.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 示 "to show himself" v.1. 使 "to employ". Before 榮 one reading has 其 "within his splendour". Instead of 榮 "splendour" v.1. 名 "glory".</div>
