# Ch. 12 How to keep off desires.

The five colours make man's eyes blind.

> <div class="commentary">Who strives greedily and lasciviously for beauty, hurts the spirits and loses enlightenment.</div>

>> <div class="note">Li Ch'iao gives a variant which adds the sentence: He is not able to behold the beauty of the not beautiful.</div>

The five notes make man's ears deaf.

> <div class="commentary">If one likes to hear the five notes, then the harmonious atmosphere flees, and the heart is not able to listen to the sounds of soundlessness.</div>

The five tastes cause man's mouth to lose.

> <div class="commentary">爽 shuang = 亡 wang to lose. If one delights in the five tastes, the mouth loses. This means that it loses Tao.</div>

>> <div class="note">For 亡 v.1. 忘 to forget. For "it loses Tao" v.1. "it loses the taste of Tao". Li Ch'iao points to the linguistic impossibility of explaining shuang by wang, as shuang is a word from the Ch'u dialect meaning "spoilt"; see Erkes in Artibus Asiae V (1935), p. 296.</div>

Chasing and hunting makes man's heart mad.

> <div class="commentary">Man's spirits like peace and quietude. Through chasing and panting the spirits become disturbed. Therefore they are maddened.</div>

Treasures difficult to obtain make man's behaviour hurtful.

> <div class="commentary">妨 = 傷 to hurt. Treasures difficult to obtain are gold, silver, pearls and jewels. Then the behaviour becomes hurtful and the body disgraced.</div>

>> <div class="note">Li Ch'iao remarks that in the Che-yao the words "one does not know satisfaction" are missing.</div>

Therefore the saint takes care of his belly.

> <div class="commentary">He cherishes the five feelings and does away with the six affections, moderates the will and nourishes the spirits.</div>

>> <div class="note">The five feelings are the feelings embodied in the five viscera, see Ts'e-yüan s.v. 五性. The six affections are joy, anger, sorrow, cheerfulness, love and hatred; comp. Ts'e-yüan s.v. 六情.</div>

He does not take care of his eyes.

> <div class="commentary">The eyes ought not to look wrong. To look wrong destroys the spirit outwardly.</div>

Therefore he flees this and chooses that.

> <div class="commentary">He flees this, the wrong looking of the eyes. He chooses that, the nourishing of the spirits within the belly.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of "spirits" v.1. "feelings".</div>
