# Ch. 54 How to cultivate insight.

Who is well-founded, will not be torn out.

> <div class="commentary">建 = 立 to found. Who by means of Tao well founds the personality and the country, cannot be disrooted and torn out.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 立 "founds" v.1. 建, meaning the same.</div>

Who is well protected, will not be taken away.

> <div class="commentary">Who by means of Tao well protects the spirits, cannot in the end be torn out and taken away.</div>

The sacrifices brought by children and grandsons will not be cut off.

> <div class="commentary">輟 = 絕 to cut off. If the sons and grandsons of a man are able to cultivate Tao in this way, they will live long and be deathless. From generation to generation they will without interruption continue to sacrifice to their ancestors.</div>

Who cultivates it in his body, his Te is able to be true.

> <div class="commentary">Who cultivates Tao within his body, saves his breath, nourishes his spirits, augments his life, accumulates his years. If his Te be such, than he will become a true man.</div>

>> <div class="note">chen-jen "true man" as a designation of the true Taoist appears first in Chuang-tse 33, 16a (SBE 40, 227), where Lao-tse and Kuan-yin-tse are thus called.</div>

Who cultivates it in his family, his Te will then be overflowing.

> <div class="commentary">When asceticism is practised within the family, when the father is kind and the son filial, when the elder brother is friendly and the younger obedient, when the husband is sincere and the wife chaste, when Te is such, then there will be abundance of happiness which is going to reach down to the sons and grandsons of coming generations.</div>

Who cultivates it in his village, his Te will be enduring.

> <div class="commentary">When Tao is cultivated within the village, when one behaves respectfully towards the elders, when one loves and nourishes the youngsters, when one educates the stupid yokels, when Te is such, then there is nothing which it did not protect.</div>

Who cultivates it in his country, his Te will be abundant.

> <div class="commentary">When Tao is cultivated within the country, then the prince will be sincere, the ministers will be true, humanity and justice will flourish spontaneously, ritual and music will develop spontaneously, and the government will be peaceful and unselfish. When Te is such, then it will be abundant.</div>

Who cultivates it within the empire, his Te will then be general.

> <div class="commentary">When the prince cultivates Tao within the empire, when he does not speak but improve, when he does not teach but govern, when the subjects really correspond to the prince like shadow and echo, when Te is such, then it is general.</div>

Therefore by means of the personality regard the personality.

> <div class="commentary">By means of a person cultivating Tao regard a person not cultivating it. Who will perish and who will remain?</div>

By means of the family regard the family.

> <div class="commentary">By means of a family not cultivating Tao regard one that cultivates it.</div>

By means of the village regard the village.

> <div class="commentary">By means of a village not cultivating Tao regard one cultivating it.</div>

By means of the country regard the country.

> <div class="commentary">By means of a country cultivating Tao regard one not cultivating it.</div>

By means of the empire regard the empire.

> <div class="commentary">By means of a prince cultivating Tao regard one who does not cultivate it.</div>

How do I know the empire to be such? By this.

> <div class="commentary">Lao-tse says: Ho do I come to know the empire? Who cultivates Tao, rises; who does not cultivate it, perishes. By this I see and know it.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1.: "By these five procedures (五事) I see and know it."</div>
