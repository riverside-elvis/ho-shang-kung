# Ch. 52 How to return to the origin.

The world has a beginning.

> <div class="commentary">The beginning is Tao.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1.: In the beginning there was Tao.</div>

It is thereby the world's mother.

> <div class="commentary">Tao is the mother of the world and of all beings.</div>

If one knows one's mother, she in turn knows her child.

> <div class="commentary">The child is unity. If one knows Tao, it must in turn know unity.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of the second "know" v.1. 立 "establish", rightly rejected by Li Ch'iao.</div>

If she knows her child, it in turn keeps to its mother.

> <div class="commentary">If [Tao] already knows unity, [unity] must in turn keep to Tao and return to non-action.</div>

To lose the body is not dangerous.

> <div class="commentary">This is not dangerous.</div>

By closing one's openings,

> <div class="commentary">The openings are the eyes. The eyes are not to look wrong.</div>

by barring one's gate,

> <div class="commentary">The gate is the mouth. Do not let the mouth speak wrong words.</div>

one finishes the body without trouble.

> <div class="commentary">Man ought to close his eyes, then he will see nothing wrong. He ought to bar his mouth, then he will say nothing wrong. Then to the end of his life he will not be overwrought.</div>

By opening one's openings,

> <div class="commentary">By opening one's eyes one beholds things desirable.</div>

by completing one's doings,

> <div class="commentary">濟 = to fill. Things filling the desires [are meant by this].</div>

one finishes the body without rescue.

> <div class="commentary">Misfortune and confusion become complete.</div>

Who beholds smallness is called enlightened.

> <div class="commentary">Though the germs do not yet move, misfortune moves them without being visible. As regards that which is small and nevertheless clear, only he who beholds it is enlightened.</div>

Who retains weakness is called strong.

> <div class="commentary">Who retains weakness and tenderness, thereby daily becomes stronger and bigger.</div>

Who uses his splendour,

> <div class="commentary">Who uses the light of his eyes within the outer world, thereby beholds the good and bad fortune of his time.</div>

who returns to his light,

> <div class="commentary">One ought to return to the light that is within. One may not allow the spirit to become negligent.</div>

>> <div class="note">One edition adds the characters 於外: "to become negligent within the outer world".</div>

will not leave the body to calamity.

> <div class="commentary">By looking inward, there is the spirit. Do not let it become lost.</div>

This is called practising the eternal.

> <div class="commentary">If a man is able to act according to this, it is called practising eternal Tao.</div>
