# Ch. 3 How to pacify the people.

Not to exalt the worthies

> <div class="commentary">By the worthies the average worthies of the world are meant. They exchange opinions, make themselves intelligible, detach themselves from Tao, adapt themselves to circumstances, avoid reality and effect appearance. Those who are not commendable may not be honoured with functions nor endowed with posts.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of "those who are not commendable" v.1. "the worthies of the world".</div>

does not cause the people to contend.

> <div class="commentary">Do not contend for merit and glory but return to nature.</div>

Not to praise treasures difficult to gain

> <div class="commentary">This means that a prince ought not to rule with a love for precious treasures. He should cast the money [back] to the mountains [where it came from] and throw the pearls and jewels into the lakes.</div>

causes people not to become thieves.

> <div class="commentary">If the superiors turn towards purity, the inferiors are not greedy.</div>

>> <div class="note">For 淨 "purity" v.1. 靜 "stillness".</div>

Not to show things desirable

> <div class="commentary">One ought to banish the songs of Cheng and to keep away adulators.</div>

>> <div class="note">The commentary is a quotation from Lun-yü 15, 10. For 侫人 "adulators" v.1. 美人 "beauties", which differs from the reading of the Lun-yü and is therefore to be rejected.</div>

does not lead the mind into confusion.

> <div class="commentary">Do not be bad and excessive nor unstable and confused.</div>

Therefore the government of the saint

> <div class="commentary">This means to govern the country together with the body.</div>

>> <div class="note">治身 "to govern the body" means in Taoist terminology the same as "to lead the life of an ascetic". We will therefore regularly translate it by "to practise asceticism".</div>

empties their minds and fills their bellies,

> <div class="commentary">Abandon desire and flee from confusion and trouble. Enclose Tao in your bosom, embrace unity and retain the five spirits.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 抱一 "embrace unity" an expression alluding to ch. 10, there is a variant 抱道 "embrace Tao", which is rejected by Li Ch'iao. On the five spirits of the bowels, see ch. 6.</div>

weakens their will

> <div class="commentary">Side with the weak and supple and do not stay with the powerful.</div>

and strengthens their bones.

> <div class="commentary">Save the semen and make its extension difficult. Then the marrow will become filled and the bones firm.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 施 "extension" v.1. 氣 "breath".</div>

He always induces the people not to know and not to desire.

> <div class="commentary">Return to simplicity and retain purity.</div>

He causes the knowing ones not to dare to act.

> <div class="commentary">Think with awe of the depth and do not take words easy.</div>

If one acts non-action,

> <div class="commentary">To do nothing sets inertness in motion.</div>

then nothing is not governed.

> <div class="commentary">If Te is changed to fulness, then the People are pacified,</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of □ "fulness" v.1. 厚, same meaning.</div>
