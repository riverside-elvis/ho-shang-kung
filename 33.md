# Ch. 33 How to discriminate Te.

Who knows men is wise.

> <div class="commentary">Who is able to know the love and hatred of men, that one is wise.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 爲智 "is wise" v.1. 有智 "has wisdom".</div>

Who knows himself is enlightened.

> <div class="commentary">If a man is able to know his own worthiness and unworthiness, this means that he reversely hears the soundless and internally perceives the shapeless. Therefore he is called enlightened.</div>

Who vanquishes others possesses strength.

> <div class="commentary">Who is able to vanquish others, only manages it by force.</div>

Who vanquishes himself is strong.

> <div class="commentary">If a man is able to vanquish the desires of his own character, then nobody in the world can fight him. Therefore he is strong.</div>

Who knows contentedness is rich.

> <div class="commentary">Who is able to know what forms the contentedness of contentedness, will long retain luck and prosperity. Therefore he is esteemed.</div>

>> <div class="note">Ho-shang-kung's reading 貴 "esteemed" presupposes a similar wording of the text he commented upon. The present reading is implied by the following variant: "Who knows contentedness, retains luck and prosperity for long. Therefore he will be rich". Wei Cheng quotes the beginning of the commentary thus: "Who is able to know what is contentedness . . .".</div>

Who forces his doings has intentions.

> <div class="commentary">Who forces himself to do good deeds has intentions on Tao. And Tao has also intentions on him.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 志 "intentions" v.1. 意 with the same meaning. The second sentence is missing in Wei Cheng's quotation. On the personal conception of Tao which here appears again, see notes on ch. 23.</div>

Who does not lose his place is permanent.

> <div class="commentary">Who is able to nourish himself by moderation, does not lose his place. By receiving the essential breath of heaven he is able to endure.</div>

Who dies without perishing is long-lived.

> <div class="commentary">If the eyes do not lose sight, if the ears do not lose hearing, if the mouth does not lose the language, then one is not hated by the empire. Therefore one is able to live long.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1s. "Therefore one has long life" and "Therefore one has no long life". The latter reading is of course wrong.</div>
