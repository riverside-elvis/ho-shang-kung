# Ch. 16 How to return to the root.

If one reaches the extreme of emptiness,

> <div class="commentary">致 = 至 the extreme. A man who has gained Tao, diminishes the feelings and drives out the desires. The five interiors are quieted down and cleared. He strives for the extreme of emptiness.</div>

>> <div class="note">The traditional reading 捐 "gives up" must be altered to 損 "diminishes", as Li Ch'iao justly remarks. The "five interiors", wu-nei 五內, are the five viscera.</div>

If one keeps quietness and firmness,

> <div class="commentary">If one keeps quietness and clarity and acts firmly.</div>

All things together rise.

> <div class="commentary">To rise is the same as to live. All things are together brought to life.</div>

We thereby see their return.

> <div class="commentary">This means: We thereby see that all things without exception return to their origin. This means that man ought to regard the origin as important.</div>

>> <div class="note">The second sentence reads in the Tao-tsang edition: Man ought to regard the origin as important.</div>

Now the full bloom of things,

> <div class="commentary">yün-yün is the fulness of blossoms and leaves.</div>

Everything returns to its root.

> <div class="commentary">This means: Everything without exception withers and dies. Everything returns to its root and then revives.</div>

To return to the root means to rest.

> <div class="commentary">Rest is called the root. The root is peaceful and pliant. Modestly it remains below. Therefore it does not return to death.</div>

This is called to return to life.

> <div class="commentary">This means: To be quiet and peaceful, this is called to return to life and thereby not to die.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 使 "and thereby" v.1. 便, meaning the same.</div>

To return to life is called lasting eternally.

> <div class="commentary">If one is able to know how to walk eternally in Tao, then one is illuminated.</div>

If one does not know the eternal, disorder causes misfortune to arise.

> <div class="commentary">Who does not know how to walk eternally in Tao, is disordered and hypocritical. Thereby he loses the spirits. Therefore he is unhappy.</div>

Who knows the eternal is called all-embracing.

> <div class="commentary">If one is able to know how to walk eternally in Tao, to abandon the feelingsand to forget the desires, then there is nothing one might not embrace.</div>

>> <div class="note">On 容 jung, here translated by "all-embracing", as Ho-shang-kung understands it, see note on ch. 15.</div>

To be all-embracing is to be universal.

> <div class="commentary">Who embraces everything, is universally just and impartial. Of the vices of the multitude, nothing agrees with him.</div>

Universality is royalty.

> <div class="commentary">Who is universally just and impartial, may thereby become king of the empire. If one justly practises asceticism, then the form becomes united to the spirits and to all beings. He collects his personality.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 身 "personality" v.1. 躬 "body".</div>

Royalty is heaven.

> <div class="commentary">If the king possesses Te, he becomes united to the spirits. Then he becomes identical with heaven.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of "if the king possesses Te" v.1.: "if one is able to be just and to be endowed with Te" and "if one is able to be king, then one possesses Te". Instead of "then he becomes identical with heaven" v.1. "then he becomes the son of heaven". Instead of the final particle 也 v.1. 矣.</div>

Heaven is Tao.

> <div class="commentary">If his Te identifies him with heaven, then he becomes one with Tao.</div>

Tao is lasting.

> <div class="commentary">After having become one with Tao one is able to last long.</div>

To lose the body is not dangerous.

> <div class="commentary">If one is able to be universal, to be king, to become identical with heaven, to become one with Tao, being these four one is pure and perfect. Without misfortune, without fault, [only] with heaven and earth entirely disappearing, one does not strive for danger and calamity.</div>
