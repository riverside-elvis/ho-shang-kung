# Ch. 36 How to become enlightened on the mystery.

If one wants to contract, one must first extend.

> <div class="commentary">Who first begins to extend wants to let his immense lust reach the utmost degree.</div>

If one wants to weaken, one must first strengthen.

> <div class="commentary">Who first begins to enlarge himself wants to meet with misfortune.</div>

If one wants to throw down, one must first raise.

> <div class="commentary">Who first raises himself wants to endanger himself through haughtiness.</div>

If one wants to take, one must first give.

> <div class="commentary">Who first gives something to himself wants to carry the desires of his heart to the utmost limit.</div>

This is called becoming enlightened on the mystery.

> <div class="commentary">These four modes of action would seem to represent the mystery of the Tao and the effective enlightenment.</div>

Tenderness and weakness conquer hardness and strength.

> <div class="commentary">Tenderness and weakness endure. Hardness and strength perish first.</div>

Fishes cannot escape from a lake.

> <div class="commentary">Fishes escaping from a lake illustrate the flight from strength and the attainment of weakness. One cannot return to the rule.</div>

The country's sharp tools ought not to be shown to the people.

> <div class="commentary">The sharp tools are the balance of Tao.</div>
