# Ch. 28 How to return to simplicity.

By knowing one's femininity and retaining one's masculinity one becomes the ravine of the empire.

> <div class="commentary">Masculinity exemplifies the exalted, femininity the subordinate. Though men know the exalted of itself, they again retain it by means of the subordinate. If one flees from the strength of masculinity, one becomes united to the weakness of femininity. Who has reached this, to him the empire turns like water gushing into a deep ravine.</div>

>> <div class="note">The last sentence alludes to Meng-tse 1, I, 7, 6.</div>

If one becomes the empire's ravine, eternal Te does not depart.

> <div class="commentary">If man is able to stay modestly below, like a deep ravine, then Te is always there. It does not again depart from the self.</div>

One returns to infancy.

> <div class="commentary">One always returns to the intention of becoming like an infant, so stupid and without knowledge.</div>

By knowing one's whiteness and retaining one's blackness one becomes the empire's model.

> <div class="commentary">Whiteness exemplifies enlightenment, blackness silence. Though a man may know himself as enlightened and penetrating, he must keep this state by being silent, as if he were in the darkness and invisible. If this is the case, then he may become the empire's model, and then his Te will remain forever.</div>

If one becomes the empire's model, eternal Te does not change.

> <div class="commentary">If a man is able to become the empire's model, then Te forever remains within his self and will not change again.</div>

One returns to the culmination of non-existence.

> <div class="commentary">If Te does not change, then one will be long-lived. One causes the body to return to the culmination of the infinite.</div>

By knowing one's splendour and retaining one's disgrace one becomes the valley of the empire.

> <div class="commentary">Splendour exemplifies estimation, disgrace exemplifies stainedness. Who knows that his self is held in estimation, must retain this by stainedness. If he is of this kind, then the empire will turn to him like water rushing down a valley.</div>

If one becomes the empire's valley, then eternal Te remains.

> <div class="commentary">足 = 止 to remain. If a man is able to become the empire's valley, then eternal Te remains within his self.</div>

One returns to simplicity.

> <div class="commentary">One must induce one's personality to return to reality and simplicity and not to outward appearance.</div>

When unwrought wood is cut up, it is turned into tools.

> <div class="commentary">Tools are useful. By cutting up all things one turns them into tools for use. When Tao is cut up, then it forms spirits. When it dissolves, it becomes sun and moon. When it is divided, the five elements are created.</div>

>> <div class="note">The first sentence "Tools are useful" is missing in one edition.  
--- What is said about Tao refers to meditative experiences.</div>

If the saint uses them, then they become the foremost of the officials.

> <div class="commentary">If the saint raises them into use, they become the foremost of all officials.</div>

Therefore a great government does not hurt.

> <div class="commentary">If a saint uses them, then he governs the empire by means of the great Tao. Thereby he hurts nothing. If one practises asceticism, then one is to govern and to quiet the desires by means of the great Tao and not to hurt the spirits.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of the second "great Tao" v.1. "the Tao of heaven", which is probably to be rejected, as it disturbs the parallelism of the sentences.</div>
