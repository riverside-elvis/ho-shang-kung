# Ch. 72 How to love the self.

If the people do not dread the dreadful, the great dreadful will reach them.

> <div class="commentary">The great dreadful is misfortune. If man does not dread small misfortune, great misfortune will befall him. Great misfortune means to die and to perish. Who dreads this must love the spirits, receive [the commands of] heaven and obey to earth.</div>

>> <div class="note">Variant: "Save the semen, nourish the spirits, obey to heaven and receive [the commands of] heaven."  
---The last expression alludes to Yi-ching, Hex. 2 (Wilhelm, I-ging, II, 14) where it is said of earth: 乃順乘天 "obediently it then receives of heaven".</div>

Do not narrow their dwelling.

> <div class="commentary">This means that the heart serves as dwelling of the spirits. One ought to enlarge and not to narrow it.</div>

Do not oppress their lives.

> <div class="commentary">Whereby man lives, this is by his possessing the spirits. The spirits rely on emptiness and enjoy stillness. If one does not restrict food and drink, one despises Tao. By thinking of beauty and evil, by filling one's stomach and spoiling one's fortune one disperses the spirits.</div>

>> <div class="note"> Instead of "the spirits rely on . . ." v.1. 不重精神 "if one does not oppress the spirits, they rely on. . .". In the Tao-tsang edition 精神 is missing, so the meaning is: "Rely on. . .". Instead of 辟 "evil" the merely orthographical variant 僻. Instead of 命 "fortune" v.1. 本 "basis". Instead of 敬 "disperses" v.1. 厭 "oppresses".</div>

Now because they are not oppressed they do not oppress.

> <div class="commentary">A man who alone does not feel the spirits as oppressive, cleans his heart, washes off the dust, is insensitive and without desires. Therefore the spirits remain within him without oppressing him.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 淡泊 "insensitive" v.1. 恬泊 "quiet". Instead of 無欲 "without desires" v.1. 無常欲 "without continual desires".</div>

Therefore the saint knows himself.

> <div class="commentary">He himself knows whereby his self gains and loses.</div>

He does not make himself visible.

> <div class="commentary">He does not outwardly display the beauty of his Te but hides it within himself.</div>

He loves his self.

> <div class="commentary">He loves his own body. Thereby he saves semen and breath.</div>

He does not esteem his self.

> <div class="commentary">He does not himself esteem a high and valued name within the world.</div>

Therefore he flees from that and seizes this.

> <div class="commentary">He flees from that displaying and valuing of himself. He takes to this knowing and loving of himself.</div>
