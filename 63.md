# Ch. 63 How to think of the beginning.

Act non-action.

> <div class="commentary">If you follow this, it will be completed. Therefore do nothing.</div>

Be occupied without occupation.

> <div class="commentary">Prepare everything beforehand. You will save trouble and reduce occupation.</div>

>> <div class="note">Li Ch'iao rejects a different reading which runs thus: Not having prepared anything beforehand, you will be occupied with saving trouble.</div>

Taste tastelessness.

> <div class="commentary">By thinking deeply and beeing remotely afraid, you will taste the meaning of Tao.</div>

Enlarge smallness. Augment minuteness.

> <div class="commentary">By regulating injunctions and orders one wants to turn the large into the small and what is much into what is little. This is the natural way.</div>

Requite hatred with Te.

> <div class="commentary">Cultivate Tao, follow the good, detach yourself from misfortune when it is still unborn.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 修道 "cultivate Tao" v.1. | 身 "practise asceticism". After 行善 "follow the good" one edition repeats 修道 "cultivate Tao". Instead of 未生 "when it is still unborn" v.1. 人生 "within the life of man", rejected by Li Ch'iao. The entire wording of Ho-shang-kung seems to be corrupt, as it does not fit in with the text. Yang Tseng-hsin, Lao-tse je-chi 5, 64a, remarks that it would presuppose a reading 防怨以德 "avert hatred by Te".</div>

Plan the difficult within the easy.

> <div class="commentary">If you want to plan difficult things, you ought to do it when they are easy, that is when they are not yet completed.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 當 "you ought" v.1. 先 "preceed with". Instead of 易時 "when they are easy" v.1. 其 | "at the right time".</div>

Do the great within the small.

> <div class="commentary">If you want to do great deeds, you must do them when little misfortune and confusion will come out of the small.</div>

The difficult affairs of the empire surely originate from the easy ones. The great affairs of the empire surely originate from the small ones.

> <div class="commentary">This illustrates what has been said above on planning.</div>

>> <div class="note">This sentence is missing in the Tao-tsang edition.</div>

Therefore the saint to the end does not play the great.

> <div class="commentary">He modestly stays within the void.</div>

>> <div class="note">This sentence is also missing in the Tao-tsang edition.</div>

Therefore he may complete his greatness.

> <div class="commentary">The empire will unite and return to him.</div>

Now to consent easily is certainly not very sincere.

> <div class="commentary">One does not put stress on the words.</div>

Much easiness surely contains many difficulties.

> <div class="commentary">One is incautious in regard to misfortunes.</div>

Therefore the saint behaves as if he took it serious.

> <div class="commentary">If the saint wants to undertake something, he behaves as if he were proceeding and receding and taking it serious. He wants to obstruct its source.</div>

Therefore to the end he has no difficulties.

> <div class="commentary">The saint to the end of his life has no sorrowful and difficult affairs, because he avoids the depths of misfortune.</div>
