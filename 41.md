# Ch. 41 How to be identical and different.

When a superior scholar hears about Tao, he tries to follow it.

> <div class="commentary">When a superior scholar hears about Tao, he earnestly exerts his energy to follow it.</div>

When a mediocre scholar hears about Tao, he now keeps it and now loses it.

> <div class="commentary">When a mediocre scholar hears about Tao, he practises asceticism in order to last long. He governs a country by means of the great peace, and so he enjoys it and remains with it. But when he again beholds riches, beauty, splendour and honours, then he has doubts about his feelings, becomes full of desires and will again lose it.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 長存 "to last long" v.1. | 生 "to live long".</div>

When an inferior scholar hears about Tao, he loudly laughs at it.

> <div class="commentary">An inferior scholar has a great many desires. If he beholds Tao to be weak, he calls it afraid. If he beholds Tao to be simple, he calls it vulgar. Therefore he loudly laughs at it.</div>

If it would not be laughed at, it would not suffice to be Tao.

> <div class="commentary">If it were not ridiculed by inferior scholars, it would not be sufficient to be called Tao.</div>

Therefore one who arranged words has it thus:

> <div class="commentary">建 = to arrange. He arranged the words to get at Tao. This corresponds to the following sentences.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1.: "to the following six sentences".</div>

To throw light on Tao is like obscuring it.

> <div class="commentary">A man who throws light on Tao resembles one who obscures it. There is nothing which he denies.</div>

To approach Tao is like retiring from it.

> <div class="commentary">To approach Tao and seize it is like retiring and not reaching it.</div>

To equalize Tao is like differentiating it.

> <div class="commentary">夷 = to equalize. A man of great Tao is not different from it like one who compares it to many other things.</div>

Superior Te is like a valley.

> <div class="commentary">A man of superior Te is like a deep valley. He is not ashamed of dirt and lowliness.</div>

Great whiteness is like spotted.

> <div class="commentary">A man of great whiteness is as if he were spotted by dirt. He is not himself resplendent.</div>

Wide Te is like insufficient.

> <div class="commentary">A man widely and grandly walking in Te seems stupid, ignorant and insufficient.</div>

Piled up Te is like stolen.

> <div class="commentary">A man piling up Te looks as if he could be robbed and emptied of them.</div>

Natural simplicity is like inconstant.

> <div class="commentary">A simple man resembles a colour which has faded and shines no more.</div>

A great square is without corners.

> <div class="commentary">A man entirely upright is neither crooked nor strict.</div>

>> <div class="note">Ho-shang-kung takes 方 "square" in the sense of 方正 "upright" and 隅 "corner" as meaning 廣隅 "strict".</div>

A large vessel is tardily completed.

> <div class="commentary">A very able man resembles [one of] the nine tripods. A sacrificial vessel cannot become quickly completed.</div>

A great note has little sound.

> <div class="commentary">A great note is like rolling thunder. It bides its time, and then it goes off. This symbolizes a man who always saves his breath and talks little.</div>

A great idea is without form.

> <div class="commentary">An entirely ideal man is simple and without appearance.</div>

Tao is hidden and without a name.

> <div class="commentary">Tao is hidden and effects that men are unable to point out its name.</div>

Now as Tao is beneficial, it is liberal and then perfect.

> <div class="commentary">成 = to perfect. This means that Tao well provides for the spirit of man and perfects it.</div>
