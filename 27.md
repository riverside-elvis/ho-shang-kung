# Ch. 27 On the use of cleverness.

Who walks well leaves neither rut nor track.

> <div class="commentary">Who walks well in Tao looks for it within his body; he does not descend into the hall nor pass through the gate. Therefore he leaves neither rut nor track.</div>

Who talks well receives neither blame nor reproach.

> <div class="commentary">To talk well means to utter well-chosen words. Then one is neither blamed nor reproached within the world.</div>

Who counts well needs no abacus.

> <div class="commentary">Who by means of Tao well calculates his business, retains unity and does not change. He does not make many calculations, therefore he needs no abacus and is nevertheless able to know.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 籌算 "abacus" v.1. | 策 "calculation".</div>

Who closes well needs no bolts and bars, and one cannot open.

> <div class="commentary">Who closes well with Tao, calms his desires and retains his semen, is not like a door which has bolts and bars and can nevertheless be opened.</div>

Who ties well needs no knotted cords, and one is nevertheless not able to untie.

> <div class="commentary">Who with Tao well ties up his business is able to tie up his mind. This is not like a knotted cord which can be untied.</div>

Therefore the saint always benignly saves men.

> <div class="commentary">Whereby the saint always teaches men, this is faithfulness and piety. Thereby he wants to save the lives of men.</div>

Therefore there are no rejected men.

> <div class="commentary">He employs the high and the low ones, and everybody gets his place.</div>

Always benignly he saves the creatures.

> <div class="commentary">What the saint always teaches the people is how to accomodate themselves to the seasons. Thereby he saves all creatures from becoming hurt.</div>

Therefore there are no rejected creatures.

> <div class="commentary">The saint does not despise the stone nor esteem the jade. He regards them as if they were one and the same.</div>

This is called conforming to the light.

> <div class="commentary">The saint benignly saves men and creatures. This is called to conform to the enlightened way of heaven.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of "the way of heaven" v.1. 大也 "is great", which makes no good sense.</div>

Therefore the good man is the teacher of the bad man.

> <div class="commentary">If men try to behave well, the saint will thereby become their teacher.</div>

The bad man is the tool of the good man.

> <div class="commentary">資 = useful. If men behave badly, the saint teaches and leads them, so to say, and makes them good. Thereby he achieves to impart usefulness to them.</div>

If one does not revere one's teacher,

> <div class="commentary">If one is alone and helpless.</div>

if one does not love one's tool,

> <div class="commentary">If one has nobody to instruct.</div>

though wise, one is very confused.

> <div class="commentary">Though they themselves think their words wise, such men are very confused.</div>

This is called mysterious.

> <div class="commentary">If one is able to penetrate the meaning of this, this means that one knows the mysterious Tao.</div>
