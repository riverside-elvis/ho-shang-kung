# Ch. 40 How to abstain from use.

Subversion is Tao's movement.

> <div class="commentary">Subversion is the root. The root is that by which Tao moves. Movement generates all things. If they turn their backs to it, then they perish.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1.: This throws light on consideration. Subversion means to choose the overthrow of tradition and to unite to justice. To overthrow tradition and to become united to justice means the saint's adapting himself to conditions. Adaptation to conditions is the movement of Tao. Therefore it is said: Subversion is Tao's movement.</div>

Weakness is Tao's use.

> <div class="commentary">Tenderness and weakness are what Tao always uses. Therefore it is able to last long.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1.: This throws light on reality. Weakness chooses tenderness and stillness. Stillness is the saint's remaining within reality. To stay within reality is the eternal use of Tao. Therefore it is said: Weakness is Tao's use.</div>

Within the world all things originate from existence.

> <div class="commentary">All things within the world are produced by heaven and earth. Heaven and earth give form and place. Therefore it is said that they take their origin from existence.</div>

>> <div class="note">Ho-shang-kung's commentary gives the impression that the text of the Tao-te-ching originally had the wording 天地萬物 "heaven, earth and all things" instead of the present reading 天下萬物 "all things within the world".</div>

Existence originates from non-existence.

> <div class="commentary">Heaven and earth, the spirits and everything flying and creeping originate from Tao. Tao is formless. Therefore they are said to originate from non-existence. This means that the fundamental vanquishes the external, that weakness vanquishes strength, and humility self-contentedness.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 形 "form" v.1. | 象 which has the same meaning.</div>
