# Ch. 53 How to complete the evidence.

If I with so much knowledge walk within the great Tao,

> <div class="commentary">介 = 大 great. Lao-tse thought the princes of his time to be sick, as they did not cross the great pass. Therefore he spoke thus: However much knowledge of government affairs I may have, I walk within the great Tao and shall deal with them through the effects of non-action.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 主 "princes" v.1. 王 "kings". Instead of 此言 "he spoke thus" v.1. 言 "he said". Instead of 躬行 "deal with them" v.1. 躬, which makes no sense. By the great pass Tao is meant.</div>

only display is to be feared.

> <div class="commentary">唯 = alone. I am afraid of having alone what I display. I am afraid to lose the meaning of Tao. I want to esteem goodness. I am afraid of simulating a good life. I wish for sincerity and loyalty. I am afraid of false loyalty arising.</div>

>> <div class="note">The explanation of 唯 by "alone" is linguistically inadmissible, see note on ch. 21.</div>

Great Tao is very smooth.

> <div class="commentary">夷 = smooth.</div>

But the people love bypaths.

> <div class="commentary">Bypaths are bad and not smooth and straight. Tao is quite smooth, but the people like to follow the bypaths which are bad and neither smooth nor straight.</div>

>> <div class="note">徑 "the bypaths" is wanting in some editions.</div>

When the palace is very secluded,

> <div class="commentary">When high terraces are erected and pavilions and houses set up.</div>

when the fields are very weedy,

> <div class="commentary">When agricultural business is neglected and the time of ploughing missed.</div>

when the granaries are entirely empty,

> <div class="commentary">When to the detriment of the country the grain is not stored.</div>

when one wears ornamented silk gowns,

> <div class="commentary">When one loves deceit and fraud and esteems externals.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 貴外華 "esteems externals" v.1. | 肖 |. The meaning remains the same.</div>

when one wears sharp swords at the belt,

> <div class="commentary">When one puts stress on strength and war and is prodigal besides.</div>

when one commits excesses in eating and drinking,

> <div class="commentary">When one wants much and there is never a time when one has enough.</div>

when one has superfluous riches --- this is the boasting of a robber.

> <div class="commentary">When the people have not enough and the prince has too much, that is the result of robbery. By wearing ornaments, by having intercourse with prodigal men, by ignorance of death the family is destroyed, and the relatives will follow it.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 隨之 "follow it" v.1. 隨 "follow".</div>

This is really not Tao.

> <div class="commentary">If a prince behaves thus, this is certainly not Tao. The following words 也哉 ye-tsai are an expression of woe.</div>

>> <div class="note">也 is wanting in one edition, probably by mistake.</div>
