# Ch. 50 How to esteem life.

To step out into life means to enter death.

> <div class="commentary">To step out into life means to want to step out of the interior. The spiritual soul is quiet, the animal soul is peaceful. Therefore this is life. To enter death means to want to enter the feelings. The spirit is troubled and full of doubts. Therefore this is death.</div>

The companions of life are thirteen. The companions of death are thirteen.

> <div class="commentary">This means that of categories of life and death there are thirteen of each. They are called the nine openings and the four bars. These mean life. When the eyes do not see wrong, the ears do not hear wrong, the nose does not smell wrong, the mouth does not talk wrong, the tongue does not taste wrong, the hands do not grasp wrong, the feet do not walk wrong, the semen is not wrongly shed, this is the contrary of death.</div>

>> <div class="note">The "four bars" are the ears, the eyes, the nose and the mouth, according to the "Old Commentary" on Wen-tse 9,8b.</div>

Of deadly spots in the man striving for life there are also thirteen.

> <div class="commentary">If man strives for life, he sets the thirteen deadly spots of subversion in motion.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 人欲求 v.1. | 之 |. The meaning remains the same.</div>

Now for what reason?

> <div class="commentary">He asks for what reason he sets the thirteen deadly spots in motion.</div>

By his living the fulness of life.

> <div class="commentary">That he sets the deadly spots in motion happens through his striving for life. Great fulness is opposed to Tao and resists heaven, goes wrong and loses the thread.</div>

For I heard: who well nourishes life,

> <div class="commentary">攝 (lit. to lead) = to nourish.</div>

when walking on land does not meet rhinoceroses or tigers.

> <div class="commentary">His nature commands him to keep away from hurtful things and not to strive for them.</div>

>> <div class="note">This explanation is missing in one edition.</div>

When going to war he does not avoid the weapons.

> <div class="commentary">He does not like to fight in order to kill men.</div>

The rhinoceros has no place where to insert its horn. The tiger has no place where to thrust in its claws. The weapon has no place where to infix its blade.

> <div class="commentary">As to a person nourishing his life, rhinoceros and tiger have no possibility of wounding him, and weapons have no possibility of transfixing him.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 從 "possibility" v.1. 由, with the same meaning.</div>

Now for what reason?

> <div class="commentary">He asks why rhinoceros, tiger and weapons are not hurtful to him.</div>

Because he has no deadly spots.

> <div class="commentary">Because he does not subvert the thirteen deadly spots. It is said that if the spirits protect him, these things of course do not dare to harm him.</div>

>> <div class="note">The characters 自然 "of course" are missing in the Tao-tsang edition.</div>
