# Ch. 14 How to praise the mysterious.

When looking at it one does not behold it. Its name is I.

> <div class="commentary">What is without colour is called I. This means: An invisible colour can neither be perceived nor seen.</div>

When listening to it one does not hear it. Its name is Hsi.

> <div class="commentary">What is without sound is called Hsi, This means: An inaudible sound can neither be caught nor heard.</div>

When grasping it one does not obtain it. Its name is Wei.

> <div class="commentary">What is without form is called Wei. This means: An unformed body can neither be grasped nor obtained.</div>

These three cannot be investigated and explored.

> <div class="commentary">These three are called I, Hsi and Wei. What cannot be explored is called the invisible, the inaudible and the unformed. Orally one cannot talk about it, in writing one cannot comment on it. One has to accept it with the mind and to investigate into it by means of the spirit. One cannot searchingly ask for it in order to obtain it.</div>

Therefore they are united and form a unity.

> <div class="commentary">混 = 合 united. Therefore they unite into something which has three names and yet forms a unity.</div>

Its upper part is not light.

> <div class="commentary">This means: If unity is above heaven, it is not bright and radiant.</div>

Its lower part is not dark.

> <div class="commentary">This means: If unity is below heaven, it is not dark [but] shows dark spots.</div>

>> <div class="note">A variant adds: Bright Tao is above and below without limits. In the Tao-tsang edition the words "It is above and below without limits" are added to the preceding explanation.</div>

In uninterrupted continuation it cannot be named.

> <div class="commentary">In uninterrupted continuation it moves without limits. What cannot be named has no colour. Blue, yellow, red, white and black cannot be distinguished. It has no sound. The five notes cannot be heard. It has no form. Longness and shortness, largeness and smallness cannot be measured.</div>

It reverts to nothingness.

> <div class="commentary">The things are material. It reverts to the state of immateriality.</div>

This is called the formation of the formless,

> <div class="commentary">This means: Unity is without form but able to cause all things to become formed.</div>

The representation of nothingness.

> <div class="commentary">Unity is without form and materiality and nevertheless produces the representations of all things.</div>

This is called abstruse and ecstatic.

> <div class="commentary">Unity is abstruse and ecstatic, as if it were existing and not existing. One cannot behold it.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 不可見之 "one cannot behold it" v.1. 不可得見, with the same meaning, and 不可狀 "it cannot be formed".</div>

When approaching it you cannot see its head.

> <div class="commentary">As unity has neither a beginning nor an end, it cannot be expected beforehand. By eliminating the feelings and making away with the desires one reverts to it.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 之 "to it" v.1. 己 "to oneself", a reading doubtless to be rejected though put by Li Ch'iao into the text.</div>

When following it you cannot see its posterior.

> <div class="commentary">This means: One cannot see the traces of the formless.</div>

By holding fast to the way of antiquity one governs the existence of the present.

> <div class="commentary">A saint holds fast to unity which begot the ways of antiquity. Through governing things he knows that the present must have unity.</div>

To be able to know the beginning of antiquity is called Tao's thread.

> <div class="commentary">Man is able to know the beginning of highest antiquity. If he possesses unity, this is called knowledge of the leading thread of Tao.</div>
