# Ch. 44 How to set up commandments.

Name or body, which is nearer?

> <div class="commentary">If the name follows, then the body retreats.</div>

Body or wealth, which is more?

> <div class="commentary">If riches are manifold, then they will harm the body.</div>

To win or to lose, what hurts more?

> <div class="commentary">If you strive to win riches, this is hurtful to your doings.</div>

Intense love is surely great waste.

> <div class="commentary">If one loves a beauty with intensity, one wastes spirituality. If one loves wealth with intensity, one gets into misfortune. What one loves, is inferior. What one loses, is important. Therefore this is called great waste.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 禍患 "misfortune" v.1. 患害, same meaning. Instead of 愛者 "what one loves" v.1. 愛甚 "what one loves with intensity".</div>

To hide much is surely a heavy loss.

> <div class="commentary">When alive, to hide much in the treasury, when dead, to hide much in the grave-mound, this during life brings about the sorrow of being robbed, in death the sorrow of having the mound dug out and the coffin searched.</div>

Who knows contentedness will not be disgraced.

> <div class="commentary">A man who knows contentedness keeps away from advantage, avoids desires and thereby experiences no humiliation done to his person.</div>

Who knows when to stop is not endangered.

> <div class="commentary">Who knows himself to be able to stop, that one will stop. If wealth and profit do not enmesh the body, if sound and colour do not confuse the ears and eyes, then the body is not endangered.</div>

Thereby he is able to last long.

> <div class="commentary">If one is able to know when to stop and how to be content, then luck and gain stay within oneself. If one practises asceticism, the spirits are not troubled. If one governs a country, the people are not bothered. Therefore one is able to last long.</div>
