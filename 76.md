# Ch. 76 How to beware of strength.

When man is born, he is tender and weak.

> <div class="commentary">The life of man contains the harmonious breath and holds the spirit fast. Therefore he is tender and weak.</div>

In death he is hard and strong.

> <div class="commentary">When a man dies, the harmonious breath becomes exhausted, and the spirit is lost. Therefore he is hard and strong.</div>

All beings, herbs and trees, are tender and frail when they are born.

> <div class="commentary">The harmonious breath remains.</div>

When dying, they are rigid and withered.

> <div class="commentary">The harmonious breath escapes.</div>

Therefore the firm and strong ones are companions of death, the tender and weak ones are companions of life.

> <div class="commentary">If one regards this by means of the two situations mentioned above, one knows that the strong one dies and the weak one will live.</div>

Therefore, when arms are strong, they will not be victorious.

> <div class="commentary">Though one may have strong weapons: if one takes war lightly and enjoys killing, the poison will spread, hatred will concentrate, and thus all the weak ones will form a strong unit. Therefore one will not be victorious.</div>

>> <div class="note">雖 "though" is missing in one edition.</div>

When trees are strong, they unite.

> <div class="commentary">When a tree is strong and big, then branches and leaves are generated together.</div>

The strong and big ones stay below. The tender and weak ones stay above.

> <div class="commentary">To cause beings to originate creates merit. Big trees are deeply rooted. It is the way of heaven to oppress the strong ones and to help the weak ones. This is the effect of naturalness.</div>
