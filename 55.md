# Ch. 55 On the charm of the mystery.

Who holds the fulness of Te in his mouth,

> <div class="commentary">This means one who holds the fulness of Te in his mouth and bosom.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 者 v.1.: 也: This means that the fulness of Te is held etc.</div>

may be compared to an infant.

> <div class="commentary">The spirits protect a man holding the fulness of Te in his mouth like parents protecting a child.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 此若 "like" v.1. 若, with the same meaning.</div>

Poisonous vermin does not sting.

> <div class="commentary">Bees, scorpions and serpents do not hurt him,</div>

Wild beasts do not claw. Birds of prey do not grip.

> <div class="commentary">An infant does not hurt any creature, nor does a creature hurt it. Therefore in an entirely peaceful generation men are neither esteemed nor despised. They all have humane hearts. Therefore stinging creatures reverse their nature, and poisonous vermin does not hurt men,</div>

The bones are weak, the sinews are tender, but the grip is tight.

> <div class="commentary">The sinews and bones of an infant are weak and tender, but it tightly grips things because its intention is bent on them and its mind does not change.</div>

It does not yet know the union of the female and the male, but its membrum is erect.

> <div class="commentary">An infant does not yet know the union of man and woman, but its membrum is erect. The excitement is caused by the abundance of the semen.</div>

It cries the whole day without becoming hoarse. This is the perception of harmony.

> <div class="commentary">An infant cries from morning till evening without its voice becoming changed. This ease is caused by the abundance of harmonious breath.</div>

To know harmony is called eternal.

> <div class="commentary">If a man is able to know the tenderness of the harmonious breath, this is of use to him. Then he knows Tao's eternity.</div>

To know the eternal is called enlightened.

> <div class="commentary">If a man is able to know the eternal circulation of Tao, then he will day for day become more enlightened and will penetrate the dark mystery.</div>

By fulfilling life one becomes daily more happy.

> <div class="commentary">Happiness endures. To fulfil life means daily to increase the wish for long life.</div>

>> <div class="note">The Tao-tsang edition reads 曰 "is called" instead of 日 "daily", thus giving the meaning: "To fulfil life is called happiness", as Wang Pi gives the text and as it is quoted by Huai-nan-tse 12, 9b. But Ho-shang-kung's explanation seems to presuppose the text as it stands and is given by Li Ch'iao. The same is the case with the following sentence.</div>

The heart causes the breath to become daily stronger.

> <div class="commentary">The heart must especially harmonize tenderness, then the breath really dwells in it. Therefore the appearance is tender. But if, contrary to this, wrong things are caused to be done, then the harmonious breath disappears from the interior. Therefore the body becomes daily harder.</div>

>> <div class="note">See the foregoing explanation.  
---The Tao-tsang edition closes the chapter here and begins ch. 56 with the following sentence.</div>

Things grow and then become old. This is called without Tao.

> <div class="commentary">When things have reached the acme of growth, then they wither and become old. That which is withered and old has not reached Tao.</div>

>> <div class="note">則 "that" and 枯 "withered" are missing in one edition.</div>

Without Tao one soon ends.

> <div class="commentary">Who does not reach Tao, ends soon by death.</div>
