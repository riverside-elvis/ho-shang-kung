# Ch. 78 How to trust in sincerity.

Within the world nothing surpasses water in tenderness and weakness.

> <div class="commentary">This means: water is pliable and soft. Within a round [basin] it becomes round, within a square one square. If dammed in, it stays. If let off, it moves.</div>

>> <div class="note">The first sentence is missing in one edition. Instead of 壅 "dammed in" v.1. 擁 "stopped up", rejected by Li Ch'iao.  
--- Comp. notes on ch. 8.</div>

But when it attacks what is firm and strong, nothing is able to vanquish it.

> <div class="commentary">Water is able to embrace mountains, to move hills, to grind iron and to dissolve copper. But nothing is able to achieve the merit of vanquishing water.</div>

That the weak vanquishes the strong, that the pliable vanquishes the unbending,

> <div class="commentary">Water is able to annihilate fire, Yin is able to dissolve Yang. The tongue is weak, the teeth are strong, but the teeth perish before the tongue.</div>

everybody in the empire knows.

> <div class="commentary">Who knows weakness is enduring. Who keeps long to strength will be hurt.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 長 "long" v.1. 久 "permanently".</div>

[But] nobody is able to act [according to it].

> <div class="commentary">Who is ashamed of humility likes to be arrogant.</div>

Therefore a saint says:

> <div class="commentary">The following exposition is meant.</div>

Who loves the dirt of the country is called lord of the altars.

> <div class="commentary">If a prince is able to take up the dirt of his country, like the [Yang-tse-]chiang and the sea which are not opposed to the small watercourses, then he is able to protect his altars and to become lord of the united empire.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 人君 "prince" v.1. 君, same meaning. Instead of 細 "small" v.1. 小, the same. Instead of 則 "then" v.1. 卽, the same.</div>

Who loves the misfortune of the country is called king of the empire.

> <div class="commentary">If a ruler is able to take [the responsibility for] mistakes upon himself, then he himself becomes the substitute for his people. If he takes misfortune upon himself, he may possess the empire as a king.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 人君 "prince" v.1. 君 the same.</div>

Correct words seem perverted.

> <div class="commentary">These are thus the correct words. The men of the world do not know this. Therefore they think them perverted.</div>
