# Ch. 20 How to differ from the vulgar.

Cut off the learned.

> <div class="commentary">Cut off the learned because they are insincere and do not agree with the science of Tao.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 文 "science" v.1. 交 "the union with Tao".</div>

There will be no sorrow.

> <div class="commentary">If the floating blossoms are taken away, then there will be no sorrow.</div>

>> <div class="note">"Floating blossoms" means things passing and superficial.</div>

Yes and Yea, how little do they differ!

> <div class="commentary">Both are suited for answers, and how little do they differ! In morbid times, reality is despised and outwardness is valued.</div>

Good and evil, how much do they differ!

> <div class="commentary">The good man is full of admiration and praise; the bad man is full of blame and quarrelling. How much do they differ! Thus in morbid times faith and sincerity are loathed and depravity and speciousness used.</div>

What man fears

> <div class="commentary">"Man" is the Taoist. What he fears is this: he is afraid of a prince who does not cut off the learned.</div>

must be feared.

> <div class="commentary">[Such an one] associates with men of insinuating appearance and kills the humane and worthy ones.</div>

The bereaved ones may not yet be in the centre.

> <div class="commentary">Somebody says that the common men of the world are bereaved and confused and want to get near the learned ones. They occupy themselves with externals and do not yet stand in the centre.</div>

All men are excessive,

> <div class="commentary">hsi-hsi means excessive and dissolute, having many feelings and desires.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 放, v.1. 佚 same meaning. In the Tao-tsang edition the words "with many feelings and desires" are missing.</div>

as if they celebrated a great feast,

> <div class="commentary">As if they were hungrily thinking of the preparations for a great feast. That is the time when they are unsatisfied.</div>

as if they mounted the towers in spring.

> <div class="commentary">In spring, when the union of Yin and Yang penetrates every creature, men feel moved to mount the towers and to look around, as they want to be licentious.</div>

I alone am timorous, as if I had not yet felt a sign..

> <div class="commentary">I alone am so timorous and quiet as if I had not yet felt a sign of my feelings and desires.</div>

Like a baby not yet smiling.

> <div class="commentary">Like a little child not yet able to take part in the mating season of men.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1. 未能答 "not yet taking part".</div>

I run about as if I had no place where to go.

> <div class="commentary">I am running about like a poor rustic who has no place where to go.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of sheng-sheng v.1. 魁然, following a different reading of the text: "far and wide I have nowhere to go".</div>

All men have superabundance,

> <div class="commentary">All men have superabundant riches with which they boast, and superabundant knowledge with which they deceive.</div>

and I alone am as if I had sustained a loss.

> <div class="commentary">I alone am like derelict, as if I had not enough.</div>

I have an idiot's heart, indeed!

> <div class="commentary">I do not correspond to the average men, I preserve unity and do not change, as if I had the mind of an idiot.</div>

Quite ignorant, o!

> <div class="commentary">I have nothing to share.</div>

The vulgar are very bright.

> <div class="commentary">They are enlightened and penetrating.</div>

I alone am like benighted.

> <div class="commentary">Like involved in darkness.</div>

The vulgar are very perspicacious.

> <div class="commentary">Very perspicacious is to be quick and prompt.</div>

I alone am quite dull.

> <div class="commentary">Dull means unable to be cut asunder.</div>

Forlorn, o! like the sea.

> <div class="commentary">I alone am forlorn, like the floods of the [Yang-tse-]chiang and the sea, the limits of which nobody knows.</div>

Driven round as if I had no standpoint.

> <div class="commentary">I alone am driven round as if I were flying, as if I were turned round without a fixed standpoint. I should like to be in the realm of the spirits.</div>

All men without exception are of some use.

> <div class="commentary">One can do something with them.</div>

But I alone am stupid like a rustic.

> <div class="commentary">I alone do nothing, like a rustic, like one who has no pursuit.</div>

I alone differ from others.

> <div class="commentary">I alone am different from other men.</div>

But I value the nourishment from the mother.

> <div class="commentary">The nourishment is the use. The mother is Tao. I alone value the use of</div>
Tao.
