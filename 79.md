# Ch. 79 How to fulfil the contract.

When great hatred is reconciled,

> <div class="commentary">Who kills somebody, will die. Who hurts somebody, will be mutilated. Thereby adequate requital is given.</div>

There will surely be a remnant of hatred.

> <div class="commentary">Who relies on punishments will lose the inclinations of men. Certainly there will be a remnant of hatred against good people.</div>

>> <div class="note">餘 "a remnant" is missing in one edition.</div>

How can this be made good?

> <div class="commentary">This means: If a man sighs, he will lose the heart of heaven. How could this be made good by reconciliation of hatred?</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 吁 "sighs" v.1. 呼 "cries out".</div>

Therefore a saint takes the left tally,

> <div class="commentary">The saints of antiquity took the left tally to complete the contract. They had neither written books nor laws. If the notched tally corresponded to the contract, this was the credential.</div>

>> <div class="note">On the ch'i 契, tallies, and the 符, contracts, see Conrady, Die chinesischen Handschriftenund sonstigen Kleinfunde Sven Hedins in Lou-lan (1920), pp. 60 a.f.</div>

but makes no claims on others.

> <div class="commentary">They only took the tally for a credential and wanted nothing else from others.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 符為信 "the tally for a credential" v.1. | 之 | "the legitimation by the tally".</div>

Therefore he who has Te examines the tally.

> <div class="commentary">A prince who has Te examines the legitimation by the tally and examines what the people lose.</div>

It is the way of heaven to know no nepotism and always to assist the good man.

> <div class="commentary">It is the way of heaven to have no relations. It only gives to the good man, and then together with the legitimation it gives the tally.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 同 "together with" v.1. 者 "and that is he to whom he gives the tally with the examination".</div>
