# Ch. 71 How to know sickness.

To know the unknowable is exalted.

> <div class="commentary">To know Tao is called non-knowledge. Therefore it is superior Te.</div>

Not to know the knowable is sickness.

> <div class="commentary">Not to know Tao is called knowledge. This is a want of Te.</div>

Now because one is sick of sickness, therefore one is not sick.

> <div class="commentary">Now because somebody may be sick of suffering, all want to know his sickness. Then he himself is not sick.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 乃 "then" v.1. 以 "thereby".</div>

The saint is not sick because he is sick of sickness. Therefore he is not sick.

> <div class="commentary">The saint does not want to know this. He is sick of this eternal suffering. All men have this sickness. Therefore he pities men. Thereby he is not sick himself. Now the saint conceals penetrating wisdom. He communicates it to the ignorant. He wants to make the world real and simple, loyal and just. Everybody ought to keep his simple nature. The vulgar do not know the intentions of Tao and lose the [right] behaviour. They want to know about affairs. By displaying themselves they internally hurt the spirits. They shorten their lives and diminish their years.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 悲 "pities" v.1. 非此人 "he ignores these men", rightly rejected by Li Ch'iao. Instead of 遶之知 "penetrating knowledge" v.1. | | 智 "penetrating wisdom", which is not in accordance with the context.</div>
