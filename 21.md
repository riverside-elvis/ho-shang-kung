# Ch. 21 How to empty the heart.

The endurance of great Te

> <div class="commentary">孔 = great. Men of great Te are able to endure everything. They are able to take up dirt and dust and to live in humble loneliness.</div>

follows alone Tao.

> <div class="commentary">唯 = alone. Men of great Te do not follow the doings of the vulgar. They only follow Tao.</div>

>> <div class="note">The explanation of 唯 as "alone" is linguistically impossible, though many other commentators and several translators follow Ho-shang-kung's explanation. 唯是 with the object interposed is a fixed expression belonging to the pre-classic language and means nothing but; it is . . . what (see Gabelentz, Chinesische Grammatik, § 804a). The sentence means therefore; "It is Tao which they follow."</div>

Tao's relation to the beings is alone ecstatic, alone abstruse.

> <div class="commentary">Tao's relation to all beings is alone ecstatic and abstruse, as it has intercourse with their baselessness.</div>

>> <div class="note">This explanation is just as untenable. 道之為物 can only mean "Tao's being a being", and 唯 before 恍 and 惚 is only intelligible if it is again taken in its pre-classic meaning "it is". The sentence thus means: "Tao's being a being is ecstatic, is abstruce."</div>

In ecstasy and abstruseness it is an image.

> <div class="commentary">Though Tao is ecstatic and abstruse, it is within the formlessness alone the model of all beings.</div>

>> <div class="note">Ho-shang-kung's interpretation, which is in any case more than doubtful, only becomes intelligible if we assume that his text of Lao-tse had the wording 惚恍中有象, as Li Ch'iao reconstructs it. With the general reading 惚分恍分, 其中有象, which is confirmed by a quotation in Huai-nan-tse 12, 16a, it is incompatible.</div>

In ecstasy and abstruseness it is a being.

> <div class="commentary">Though Tao is abstruse and ecstatic, there is unity within it. It effects the changes, helps the breath and establishes reality.</div>

>> <div class="note">This explanation also presupposes the reading 惚恍中有物 which Li Ch'iao reconstructs, instead of the current text 恍分惚分, 其中有物.</div>

In deepness and darkness it has the essence.

> <div class="commentary">Tao is only deep, dark and formless; within itself it has got the essence. Surely the spirits press each other, and Yin and Yang unite with each other.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 道唯 "Tao is only" Li Ch'iao reads 道德 "Tao and Te" which is surely to be rejected. Instead of the final 也 the Tao-tsang edition has 之.</div>

Its essence is very real.

> <div class="commentary">This means: The atmosphere of the existing reality, its secret is very real and without appearance.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of "the existing reality" v.1. "the reality of Tao".</div>

Within itself it has truth.

> <div class="commentary">Tao conceals its merit and hides its glory. Its truth is within itself.</div>

From antiquity till today its name does not vanish.

> <div class="commentary">It follows itself. From antiquity till today Tao eternally exists and does not vanish.</div>

Thereby it takes care of the beginning of everything.

> <div class="commentary">閱 = to take care of. 甫 = beginning. Tao takes care of the beginning life of all beings. If one follows Tao, one preserves the breath.</div>

How do I know that this is the case with the beginning of everything?

> <div class="commentary">How do I come to know that all beings preserve their breath if they follow Tao?</div>

>> <div class="note">In the Tao-tsang edition the commentary accompanying this sentence is wanting; in other editions the words "all beings" are left out.</div>

By this.

> <div class="commentary">"This" is the present (今). By the present all beings receive the breath of Tao and live and thrive. Without Tao this would not be the case.</div>

>> <div class="note">This commentary is also wanting in the Tao-tsang edition. In the last sentence, "Tao" is probably a mistake for "the present".  
---Comp: the same phrase in ch. 57.</div>
