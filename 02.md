# Ch. 2 How to cultivate the personality.

If all in the world know that beauty is beautiful,

> <div class="commentary">Beauty developing of itself makes this apparent.</div>

then there is ugliness.

> <div class="commentary">There is danger and loss.</div>

If all know that goodness is good,

> <div class="commentary">If there is merit and glory.</div>

then badness exists.

> <div class="commentary">That for which men contend.</div>

Thus existence and non-existence generate each other.

> <div class="commentary">By beholding existence it becomes non-existence.</div>

Heaviness and lightness perfect each other.

> <div class="commentary">By beholding heaviness it becomes lightness.</div>

Longness and shortness form each other.

> <div class="commentary">By beholding shortness it becomes longness.</div>

Highness and lowness incline towards each other.

> <div class="commentary">By beholding highness it becomes lowness.</div>

Sound and voice harmonize with each other.

> <div class="commentary">If the superior sings, the inferior is sure to keep in tune with him.</div>

Before and afterwards follow each other.

> <div class="commentary">If the superior advances, the inferior is sure to follow.</div>

Therefore the saint remains in the business of non-action.

> <div class="commentary">He governs through Tao.</div>

He follows the doctrine of not-speaking.

> <div class="commentary">Through his personality he is master and leader.</div>

All things rise,

> <div class="commentary">Everything is set in motion.</div>

and they are not rejected.

> <div class="commentary">They are not rejected and do not rebel.</div>

He produces without owning.

> <div class="commentary">The pristine breath produces all things without owning them.</div>

He acts and puts no stress on it.

> <div class="commentary">This is what Tao is doing. It puts no stress on anything and does not look from afar for recompense.</div>

>> <div class="note">Li Ch'iao quotes from the Che-yao, a work of Wei Cheng 魏徵, a Taoist of the T'ang period (see Chung-kuo jen-ming ta ts'e-tien, p. 1742; Wang Chung-min, Lao-tse k'ao, pp. 134 and 184) the following variant: "This is what Tao is doing. It does not strive for such a recompense."</div>

Merit is accomplished, and he does not stay with it.

> <div class="commentary">After having perfected the accomplishing of merit, he retires. He does not stay in his place.</div>

Now because he does not stay with it,

> <div class="commentary">Now as [his] merit is accomplished, he does not stay in his place.</div>

thereby he does not flee.

> <div class="commentary">Luck and Te are constantly present and do not leave his person. This means: If one does not advance, one cannot be followed; if one does not talk, one cannot be looked through. The six preceding sentences therefore contain highness and lowness, longness and shortness. If you open one source, the hundred causes arise below. The changes of the hundred causes are sure to set confusion in motion.</div>
