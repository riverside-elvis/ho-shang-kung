# Ch. 59 How to guard Tao.

When governing the people,

> <div class="commentary">This means that the prince wants to govern the people.</div>

when making use of heaven,

> <div class="commentary">事 = 用 make use of. One must make use of the ways of heaven and comply with the four seasons.</div>

there is nothing like coveting.

> <div class="commentary">嗇 = 貪 to covet. Who governs with love, must love the wealth of the people. He must not be prodigal. Who practises asceticism, must love semen and breath. He must not let them escape.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1s. 嗇 = 愛 to love, and | = 愛惜, to use sparingly. The latter is rejected by Li Ch'iao.</div>

Now to covet means to acquire in time.

> <div class="commentary">早 = 先 in time. 服 = 得 to acquire. When the riches of the people are sparingly used, then men are peaceful. When semen and breath are sparingly used, then the Tao of heaven may be acquired in time.</div>

To acquire in time, this means to make amassed Te heavy.

> <div class="commentary">To acquire the Tao of heaven in time means to put stress on Te amassed within the self.</div>

When one makes amassed Te heavy, then nothing is invincible.

> <div class="commentary">克 = 勝 to vanquish. When stress is put on Te amassed in the self, then everything may be vanquished.</div>

When nothing is invincible, then nobody knows his limits.

> <div class="commentary">When nothing is invincible, then nobody knows that the limits of his own Te might be restricted.</div>

When nobody knows his limits, he may thereby own the country.

> <div class="commentary">When nobody knows his own Te to have limits, he thereby may own a country and make the people happy.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 國 "a country" v.1. 社稷 "the spirits of the land".</div>

When one owns the mother of the country, he may thereby last long.

> <div class="commentary">The country is identical with the body. The mother is Tao. When a man is able to protect Tao within the body, he thereby causes his breath not to become heavy and the five spirits [of the viscera] not to become troubled. Then he is able to last long.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 久 "to last" v.1. 生 "to live".</div>

This is called the deep-going root and the firm trunk.

> <div class="commentary">Man may regard the breath as the root and the semen as the trunk. When the roots of a tree do not reach deeply down, it will be disrooted. When the trunk is not firm, it will fall. This means: Hide your breath deeply, keep your semen firmly, and do not let them leak out.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 蒂 "trunk" v.1. 蔕, the same. Instead of --- "do not let them leak out" v.1. --- "they ought not to leak out".</div>

This is the way to long life and to the permanent view.

> <div class="commentary">Deep-going root and firm trunk are thus the way to long life and to the permanent view.</div>
