# Ch. 58 How to obey to changes.

When the government is very dull,

> <div class="commentary">Whenever a government is broad-minded, it seems to be dull and unenlightened.</div>

the people are very simple.

> <div class="commentary">Then the people are simple, have wealth in full and agree well with each other.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1.: They are simple and approach fulness.</div>

When the government is very observant,

> <div class="commentary">Where the government is sharp and quick, speech is discerned within the mouth and hearing within the ear.</div>

then the people are quite broken down.

> <div class="commentary">As the people are unable to maintain their living, they are completely broken down. Therefore they become daily more emaciated.</div>

Misfortune, Oh! is that upon which happiness leans.

> <div class="commentary">倚 = 因 to lean upon. Now when fortune leans upon misfortune, then it originates. When man is hit by misfortune, he is able to repent of his mistakes and to reproach himself, to cultivate goodness and to walk in Tao. Then misfortune flees and happiness approaches.</div>

>> <div class="note">In one edition, the characters "misfortune" and "happiness" are interchanged, which makes no sense.</div>

Happiness, Oh! is that wherein misfortune hides itself.

> <div class="commentary">Misfortune secretly hides within happiness. When man possesses happiness, then he becomes haughty and unbridled. Then happiness flees and misfortune approaches.</div>

Who knows their limits?

> <div class="commentary">As happiness and misfortune generate each other, who is able to know the time when they reach their limits?</div>

Their injustice?

> <div class="commentary">無 = 不 not. This means: When a prince does not justly deal with his person, then he will be without a country.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 而 "then" v.1. 其: "he may become landless".</div>

Justice becomes hypocrisy.

> <div class="commentary">奇 = 詐 hypocrisy. When a prince is not just, the subjects, though they may be just, are in turn changed into hypocrites by the prince.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 雖 "though" v.1. 譌 "fraudulent", without sense.</div>

What is good in turn becomes evil.

> <div class="commentary">All the good people turn against the prince and think him detrimental.</div>

The confusion of the people in such days is certainly of long standing.

> <div class="commentary">This means: When the prince is bewildered, he is sure to lose his right-mindedness. This time will certainly last long.</div>

Therefore the saint is straightforward and not hurtful,

> <div class="commentary">The saint's behaviour is upright; thereby he wants to lead the subjects. Therefore he does not hurt the people.</div>

incorrupt and not injurious,

> <div class="commentary">The manners of the saint are incorrupt and pure. Thereby he wants to improve the people, not to injure men. Now this is not the case. Even through justice one hurts men.</div>

Upright and not extending.

> <div class="commentary">肄 = 申 to extend. Though the saint is upright, he nevertheless bends his self, follows men and does not display himself.</div>

Splendid and not shining.

> <div class="commentary">Though the saint alone sees the light, he always seems obscured and not shining.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 獨 "alone" v.1. 燭 "radiating", rejected by Li Ch'iao.</div>
