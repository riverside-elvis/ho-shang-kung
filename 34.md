# Ch. 34 How to trust in perfection.

Tao is overflowing, O!

> <div class="commentary">This means: Tao is overflowing, like swimming, like sinking, like existing, like not existing. If one beholds it, it is not Tao. If one talks about it, it is difficult to distinguish.</div>

It may be on the left or on the right.

> <div class="commentary">Tao may be on the left or on the right. There is nothing to which it does not correspond.</div>

All beings wait for it and live.

> <div class="commentary">恃 = 待 to wait. All beings wait for it in order to live.</div>

And they are not rejected.

> <div class="commentary">Tao rejects nobody and is opposed to nothing.</div>

Merit is accomplished and exists without a name.

> <div class="commentary">The possessor of Tao has no name. His [mere] existence accomplishes merit.</div>

>> <div class="note">In one reading 成 "accomplishes" is wanting. Then it would read: "Thus he has merit".</div>

It loves and nourishes all beings and does not act as their master.

> <div class="commentary">Though Tao loves and nourishes all beings, it is not like a ruler governing them.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 牧 "governing" v.1. 放, with the same meaning.</div>

It is always without desires and may be called the Little One.

> <div class="commentary">Tao is hidden and Te concealed. The name is at anchor within non-action like subtility.</div>

All beings return, and it does not act as their master.

> <div class="commentary">All beings return to Tao and receive its breath. Tao is not like a ruler who deals out punishments.</div>

One may call it that which is great.

> <div class="commentary">All things come and go in every direction. It allows everything to be independent. Therefore it does not seem to be great.</div>

Therefore the saint to his end does not play the great one.

> <div class="commentary">The saint takes Tao for his model, hides Te and conceals the name, because they do not complete grandeur.</div>

Therefore he can accomplish his grandeur.

> <div class="commentary">The saint with his personality masters Tao. He does not talk but changes everything and practises asceticism. Thereby he is able to accomplish his grandeur.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of "Tao" v.1. 導 "the lead". Instead of 萬物 "everything" v.1. | 事 "all doings".</div>
