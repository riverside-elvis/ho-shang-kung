# Ch. 30 How to forbear war.

A ruler who leans on Tao

> <div class="commentary">This means that the ruler himself is able to lean on Tao.</div>

does not use arms to force the country.

> <div class="commentary">A ruler who leans on Tao makes no use of arms. If he is obedient to heaven, if he makes use of Te, the enemy will surrender of himself.</div>

Such doings are well requited.

> <div class="commentary">Such praiseworthy doings are well requited. If he reproaches himself, he will not be hateful to the people.</div>

Where an army stays, thistles and thorns are growing.

> <div class="commentary">Agriculture is neglected, and the fields are not cultivated.</div>

After a great war there will certainly be bad harvests.

> <div class="commentary">Heaven corresponds to this by sending bad weather that spoils the crops. The crops are [then] very hurtful to men.</div>

The good man is resolute, and that suffices.

> <div class="commentary">Who follows goodness is resolute and daring, and that is sufficient. Then he stops.</div>

>> <div class="note">The Tao-tsang edition reads: Who makes good use of arms, must be daring, and that is sufficient. He does not think this nice. Li Ch'iao gives two other readings, one running thus: "A good warrior must be resolute and daring, and that is sufficient. He does not stop (is not stopped?)." The other version is from the Che-yao: "Who follows goodness must be resolute and daring, and that is sufficient. He does not stop".</div>

He dares not thereby take to force.

> <div class="commentary">He dares not make his name great by resolutely taking to force.</div>

He is resolute and not boastful.

> <div class="commentary">He must resolutely dare to be modest and humble and not to boast and to make himself appear as great.</div>

He is resolute and not aggressive.

> <div class="commentary">He must resolutely dare to decline and not himself aggressively seize what is beautiful.</div>

He is resolute and not insulting.

> <div class="commentary">驕 (haughty) = 欺 insulting. He dares resolutely not to insult others.</div>

He is resolute and unable to act otherwise.

> <div class="commentary">He must resolutely dare to be entirely sincere. He must not be oppressive and unable to act otherwise.</div>

He is resolute and not violent.

> <div class="commentary">He resolutely dares not to apply strong weapons and firm cuirasses in order to hurt and to offend others.</div>

Things grow, then they age.

> <div class="commentary">When herbs and trees have reached the height of their strength, they wither. When man has reached the height of his strength, then he becomes weak and old. This means that strength is unable to last.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 枯槁 "wither" v.1. 落 "fall down". Instead of 不可以久 "unable to last" v.1. | | | 壯 "unable to remain in vigour".</div>

This is called to be without Tao.

> <div class="commentary">What is withered and old remains seated and does not walk in Tao.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 坐 "to remain seated" v.1. 生 "to become born", thus reading "what withers and is born old". Probably to be rejected.</div>

What is without Tao ends soon.

> <div class="commentary">Who does not walk in Tao will soon die.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 早死 "will soon die" v.1. | 亡 "will soon perish".</div>
