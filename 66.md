# Ch. 66 How to put the self behind.

That the [Yang-tse-]chiang and the sea are able to be kings of the torrents is because of their well submitting to them.

> <div class="commentary">Because the [Yang-tse-]chiang and the sea are lower, all the currents are drawn towards them, like the people turning to their king.</div>

Therefore they are able to be kings of all the torrents.

> <div class="commentary">As they are lower, they are able to become kings of all the torrents.</div>

If therefore the saint wants to be above the people,

> <div class="commentary">If he wants to have a position above the people.</div>

he by his words submits to them.

> <div class="commentary">He takes the [Yang-tse-]chiang and the sea for his models and remains in a modest position.</div>

>> <div class="note">In Li Ch'iao's edition, 處 "position" is twice misprinted 虛 "empty".</div>

If he wants to precede the people,

> <div class="commentary">If he wants to range before the people.</div>

with his personality he puts himself behind them.

> <div class="commentary">He allows others to precede and puts his self behind.</div>

When therefore the saint dwells above, the people are not weighed down.

> <div class="commentary">When a saint dwells above the people as their prince, he does not frighten his subjects by his dignity. Therefore the people bear him and look up to him. They do not feel him as a weight.</div>

When he stands forth, the people are not hurt.

> <div class="commentary">When preceding the people, the saint does not obscure the people behind him by his splendour. He loves the people as if he were their father and mother. He has no wish to hurt them.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 後民 "the people behind him" v.1. 後 "those behind him". Instead of 無有欲害之者 "he has no wish to hurt them" v.1. 無有欲害之心也 "he has no wish to hurt their feelings".</div>

Therefore the empire is glad to push him on and gets not tired of him.

> <div class="commentary">It is the saint's intention to love the people deep and strong like little children. Therefore the empire is glad and pushes him forward to make him a prince. It does not get tired of him.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 如 "like" v.1. 若, same meaning.</div>

Because he does not contend,

> <div class="commentary">The empire gets not tired of the time of a sage. This is the case because the saint does not contend with the people who shall be first or last.</div>

Therefore nobody in the empire is able to contend with him.

> <div class="commentary">This means: All men contend about action. Nobody contends with me about non-action.</div>
