# Ch. 8 How to change nature.

The most good one is like water.

> <div class="commentary">The man of highest goodness has the nature of water.</div>

Water is good and beneficent towards all beings.

> <div class="commentary">Water in the sky produces mist and dew, on the earth it produces sources and lakes.</div>

But it does not contend. It stays in places odious to man.

> <div class="commentary">All men loathe low and damp or dusty and dirty places. Water alone runs quietly on and stays there.</div>

Therefore it is akin to Tao.

> <div class="commentary">The nature of water is somewhat akin to that of Tao.</div>

For its place it chooses earth.

> <div class="commentary">The nature of water is to benefit the plants on earth. If water comes down from high, it resembles a female creature that moves in submitting to the male.</div>

For its heart it chooses the lake.

> <div class="commentary">Water is deep and empty; the lake is deep and clear.</div>

For giving it chooses humanity.

> <div class="commentary">All things receive water for to live. It adds to emptiness, it does not add to fulness.</div>

For speech it chooses sincerity.

> <div class="commentary">Within the water there are the shadows and reflexes of the light, [but by them] it does not lose its nature.</div>

For justice it chooses sincerity.

> <div class="commentary">There is nothing that is not purified and pacified.</div>

For action it chooses ability.

> <div class="commentary">It is able to be square as well as round. If bent or straightened, it accomodates itself to the form.</div>

For motion it chooses time.

> <div class="commentary">In summer it dissolves, in winter it freezes, it keeps to the terms and changes [according to them]. It never neglects its time.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 其時 "its time" v.1. 天時 "the seasons of heaven".</div>

Now because it does not contend,

> <div class="commentary">If dammed in, it stays. If let off, it flows. It follows man.</div>

therefore it is not blamed.

> <div class="commentary">Such is the nature of water. Therefore there is nobody in the world who would blame water.</div>
