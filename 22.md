# Ch. 22 How to increase humility.

If twisted, then one becomes perfect.

> <div class="commentary">Who twists himself, follows the multitude and acts not on his own responsibility. Thereby he completes his personality.</div>

>> <div class="note">The words "his personality" are missing in several editions.</div>

If crooked, then one becomes straight.

> <div class="commentary">Who makes himself crooked but straightens others, in the long run acquires straightness himself.</div>

If hollow, then it becomes filled.

> <div class="commentary">If the earth is low, the waters flow thither. If a man is humble, Te comes to him.</div>

If worn out, then one is newly mended.

> <div class="commentary">Who regards himself as worn out and poor, will afterwards precede others. The world will honour him. In the long run he himself will become renewed.</div>

If one has little, then one will receive.

> <div class="commentary">Who himself takes little, will receive much. It is the way of heaven to protect the humble ones. The spirits rely on emptiness.</div>

>> <div class="note">It is the way of heaven to protect the humble ones. If one takes little oneself, then one will receive much.</div>

If having much, one will be disappointed.

> <div class="commentary">Who has abundance of riches, has his doubts how to protect them. Who has abundance of learning, has his doubts to whom he should listen.</div>

>> <div class="note">The second sentence is missing in Li Ch'iao's text but contained in the Tao-tsang edition and quoted by Li Ch'iao from the Che-yao.</div>

Therefore the saint holds fast to unity and becomes the empire's model.

> <div class="commentary">He holds fast to the model. If the saint preserves unity, then he knows about all affairs. Therefore he is able to become the empire's model.</div>

He does not regard himself. Therefore he is enlightened.

> <div class="commentary">The saint by means of his eyes does not look beyond a thousand miles. Thus he relies on the eyes of the empire for looking out. Therefore he is able to be enlightened and penetrating.</div>

Through not existing for himself he is eminent.

> <div class="commentary">The saint does not exist himself through action but he ignores men. Therefore he can become apparent to the world.</div>

He does not take anything for himself. Therefore there is merit.

> <div class="commentary">伐 = to take. The Te of the saint effects changes and spreads. He does not claim its beauty for himself. Therefore he has merit within the empire.</div>

>> <div class="note">The explanation of fa is missing in the Che-yao.</div>

He does not himself boast. Therefore he endures.

> <div class="commentary">矜 ching = 大 to think great. The saint does not himself praise his grandeur. Therefore he is able to last without being endangered.</div>

>> <div class="note">The explanation of ching is also missing in the Che-yao.</div>

Now as he does not contend, therefore nobody within the empire is able to contend with him.

> <div class="commentary">This means: Within the empire, neither worthy nor unworthy persons are able to contend with somebody who does not contend.</div>

What was said in ancient times: 'If twisted, then one becomes perfect' is this perhaps an empty saying?

> <div class="commentary">The word transmitted from antiquity: "Who is twisted and follows, thereby completing his personality" is a correct saying and no empty nonsense.</div>

>> <div class="note">In the Tao-tsang edition, 身 "his personality" is wanting. Li Ch'iao has instead of "is a correct saying" "this saying is no empty nonsense".</div>

Truly he is perfect and returns.

> <div class="commentary">誠 = truly. Who is able to act the "twisted following", realises his body and returns to his parents. Nothing will hurt him.</div>
