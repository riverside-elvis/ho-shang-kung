# Ch. 62 How to exert Tao.

Tao is the hiding-place of all beings.

> <div class="commentary">奧 ngao is a hiding-place. Tao is the hiding-place of all beings. There is nothing which it did not contain.</div>

The treasure of the good man.

> <div class="commentary">The good man makes Tao the treasure of his person. He does not dare to depart from it.</div>

That which the bad man guards.

> <div class="commentary">Tao is what the bad man guards. If he meets with sorrow, if he is pressed by hurry, he seems to know repentance for his vileness.</div>

In fine words one may trade.

> <div class="commentary">In fine words one is only able to wade. For bartering is easy and retraction incongruous. As to fine and insinuating words, the buyer wants to acquire them quickly, and the seller wants to get quickly rid of them.</div>

By dignified behaviour one is able to differ from others.

> <div class="commentary">加 = 別 to differ, If a man knows how to behave in exalted and low conditions, he will be able to be different from all other men but not yet sufficient to venerate Tao.</div>

Though a man be bad, how could he be rejected?

> <div class="commentary">Though a man be bad, he must be improved by Tao. Surely in the times of the three Exalted Ones there was no rejection of the people. Te vastly improved them.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 以道化 "to improve by Tao" v.1. | | 伏 "to repress by Tao", which goes against the sense. Instead of 三時之皇 "in the times of the three Exalted Ones" v.1. | | | 前 "before the three Exalted Ones". Instead of □ "vastly" v.1. 厚, with the same meaning.</div>

Therefore they installed the son of heaven and appointed the three dukes.

> <div class="commentary">One wanted to improve the bad men.</div>

Though one has a jade-sceptre and takes precedence in a quadriga, this is not equal to sitting there and approaching Tao.

> <div class="commentary">Though one may have a beautiful sceptre of jade and take precedence in a quadriga, it is better to sit still and to approach Tao.</div>

Why the ancients esteemed this Tao, why was this the case? Not because they daily strove to acquire it?

> <div class="commentary">That by which the ancients esteemed this Tao was not daily roaming far in order to look for it. They found it near themselves in their persons.</div>

By having sin one avoids evil.

> <div class="commentary">To have sin means to find the world in confusion. A benighted prince falsifies the course by indulging in punishments. By cultivating Tao one may avoid all evil.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 解免 "to avoid" v.1. 死 | "avoid in death", probably a wrong reading. Instead of 衆邪 "all evil" v.1. 罪 | "sin and evil".</div>

Therefore one is honoured within the empire.

> <div class="commentary">If Tao and Te penetrate thoroughly, everything is embraced and redeemed. When completing the personality, when governing the country, one ought to be quiet and without action. Then one may be honoured within the empire.</div>
