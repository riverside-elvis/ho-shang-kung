# Ch. 7 On the dawning splendour.

Heaven and earth are enduring and lasting.

> <div class="commentary">This wants to say that heaven and earth are of long existence and permanent living, whereby they teach man.</div>

Whereby heaven and earth are able to endure and besides this to last, is through their not living for themselves.

> <div class="commentary">Whereby heaven and earth alone endure and last, is their quietness. In giving they do not expect recompense, unlike man who strives in haste to enrich himself. They use man in order to help him.</div>

Thereby they are able to live long.

> <div class="commentary">Because they do not strive for life, they are able to live long and never to end.</div>

Therefore the saint puts his person behind,

> <div class="commentary">He allows others to go ahead and puts himself last.</div>

and his person comes to the front.

> <div class="commentary">In the world he is honoured. He goes ahead and thereby becomes superior.</div>

He puts his person aside,

> <div class="commentary">He thinks little of himself and loves others.</div>

and his person remains.

> <div class="commentary">All the people love him like their father and mother. The spirits protect him like a little child. Therefore his person continues to remain.</div>

Is this not because he has no egoism?

> <div class="commentary">The saint is loved by men and protected by the spirits. Is this not because he reigns with justice and unselfishness?</div>

Thereby he is able to fulfil his egoism.

> <div class="commentary">Men by being egoistic want to achieve fulness. The saint is no egoist but achieves fulness spontaneously. Therefore he is able to fulfil his egoism.</div>
