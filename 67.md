# Ch. 67 On the three jewels.

If all within the empire call my Tao great, I seem unworthy.

> <div class="commentary">Lao-tse says: If all within the empire call my Tao great, then I pretend to be stupid and seem to be unworthy.</div>

Now because one is great, therefore one is unworthy.

> <div class="commentary">Who is only called great in Tao, that one will hurt his body. Therefore one should pretend to be stupid and seem to be unworthy. Nothing is separated and nothing cut asunder. Do not disdain others nor esteem yourself.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 唯 "only" v.1. 夫 "now". "That one" is missing in one edition.</div>

To seem to be worthy is to endure.

> <div class="commentary">Worthy is good. It means convincing. If somebody convinces others, he is esteemed himself. To run a government with much sharpsightedness has always endured.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 辯 "convincing" v.1. 辨 "discriminating", probably a mere misprint. The last sentence seems corrupt, as it expresses a view contrary to Taoist ideas; comp. ch. 58. Probably a negative particle is missing.</div>

This is really trifling.

> <div class="commentary">This means: Who convinces is only like a mean fellow and not like a superior.</div>

I have three jewels. I prize and keep them.

> <div class="commentary">Lao-tse says: I have three jewels. I prize them and keep hold of them.</div>

The first is called kindness.

> <div class="commentary">He loves the people like a little child.</div>

The second is called thriftiness.

> <div class="commentary">He raises taxes as if he took them from himself.</div>

The third is called: not daring to play the first part in the empire.

> <div class="commentary">He keeps to modesty, retires and does not play the leading part.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1.: "and does not dare to play the leading part". The Che-yao renders the commentary thus: He keeps to modesty, retires and does not play the leader.</div>

Now who is kind, therefore is brave.

> <div class="commentary">By making use of kindness and humanity one may be brave in loyalty and filial piety.</div>

Who is thrifty may therefore be large-minded.

> <div class="commentary">When the son of heaven is personally able to be large-minded, then the people daily make use of his large-mindedness.</div>

>> <div class="note">The words 天子 t'ien-tse "the son of heaven" are missing in the Che-yao. The same work reads instead of 大廣 "large-mindedness" 厚寬 "the fulness of large-mindedness".</div>

Who dares not play the first part within the empire,

> <div class="commentary">Do not want to be the head of the empire.</div>

may thereby complete the duration of the vessel.

> <div class="commentary">To complete the duration of the vessel means to be enabled to become a Taoist. I may be the first among Taoists.</div>

Today one neglects kindness and is at the same time bold.

> <div class="commentary">Now the world gives up humanity and kindness and wages war.</div>

One neglects thriftiness and is at the same time large-minded.

> <div class="commentary">One gives up thriftiness and only indulges in profusion.</div>

One neglects retirement and precedes at the same time.

> <div class="commentary">One desists from putting the self in the background and only precedes others.</div>

This is deadly.

> <div class="commentary">By behaving thus one moves along the way leading to death.</div>

Now as to kindness: By fighting with it one will vanquish. By protecting with it one will secure.

> <div class="commentary">Now as to the kind and humane one, the people will draw near to him, [and there will only be] one opinion within two hearts. Therefore by war one vanquishes, by protective measures one remains secure.</div>

Heaven wants to save him. With kindness it protects him.

> <div class="commentary">Heaven wants to help the good man. Surely it enables him to help by his kind and humane character.</div>
