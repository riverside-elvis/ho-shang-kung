# Ch. 19 How to return to purity.

Cut off the saints!

> <div class="commentary">By cutting off the government of the saints, one restores the return to the beginning and keeps to the origin. The five emperors suspended the constellations; Ts'ang Chie invented writing. They were not equal to the three August Ones who used knotted cords and did without written characters.</div>

Throw away wisdom!

> <div class="commentary">Throw away wisdom and sagacity and return to non-action.</div>

The people will be benefited a hundredfold.

> <div class="commentary">Agricultural questions ought to be publicly and not privately settled.</div>

Cut off humanity and throw away justice!

> <div class="commentary">Who cuts off humanity, is looking towards benevolence and kindness. Who throws away justice, esteems beautiful words.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of 華言 "beautiful words" v.1. | 信 "outward sincerity". The sentence makes no sense and is evidently corrupt.</div>

The people return to piety and clemency.

> <div class="commentary">Te changes into simplicity.</div>

Cut off the clever men and throw away the beneficent ones!

> <div class="commentary">Cut off the clever men; they are deceitful and create disorder among the sincere ones. Throw away the beneficent ones, so that the ways of greed may be blocked up and the gate to power barred.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1.: "Cut off the clever deceivers and block up the ways of greed".</div>

There will be no more thieves and robbers.

> <div class="commentary">If the prince reforms the government, the subjects will be without egoism and depravity.</div>

As to these three,

> <div class="commentary">This means the three things mentioned above which ought to be thrown away and cut off.</div>

to have knowledge of them is not sufficient.

> <div class="commentary">To have knowledge of them means that knowledge is not sufficient for instructing the people.</div>

Therefore let there be something on which one may rely.

> <div class="commentary">This corresponds to the following sentence.</div>

Look at simplicity and hold fast to naturalness.

> <div class="commentary">To look at simplicity corresponds to holding fast to simplicity and keeping to truth as well as to not looking at externals. To hold fast to naturalness corresponds to looking at real naturalness in order to show it to the subjects. Thereby one may become a model.</div>

>> <div class="note">V.1.: By looking at simplicity and keeping to truth one holds fast to reality and simplicity.</div>

Diminish egoism.

> <div class="commentary">To diminish egoism means to justify altruism.</div>

>> <div class="note">After the Tao-tsang edition. Li Ch'iao only has: "To justify altruism".</div>

Leave the desires alone.

> <div class="commentary">To leave the desires alone corresponds to the knowledge of having enough.</div>

>> <div class="note">Again after the Tao-tsang edition, Li Ch'iao' text runs: "This corresponds to the knowledge of having enough."</div>
