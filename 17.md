# Ch. 17 On primitive customs.

In the highest antiquity the subjects knew that they existed.

> <div class="commentary">By highest antiquity the nameless princes of highest antiquity are meant. The subjects knew that they existed [means]: the subjects knew that there was a prince above them, but they showed no trace of servile behaviour. This was the fulness of simplicity and naturalness.</div>

>> <div class="note">The Tao-tsang edition reads instead of the last sentence: "but they showed no servile behaviour but simplicity and naturalness. Li Ch'iao rejects this reading.</div>

In the next [period] they were attached to them and praised them.

> <div class="commentary">Their Te was visible, their benevolence was admired. Therefore they were loved and praised.</div>

In the next one they feared them.

> <div class="commentary">They devised laws and punishments. Thereby they ruled.</div>

In the next one they reviled them.

> <div class="commentary">They forbade many things and gave troublesome orders. It was impossible to be sincere. Therefore they were deceived and reviled.</div>

If faith is not sufficient,

> <div class="commentary">If the prince's faith towards his subjects is not sufficient, then the subjects become a nation of swindlers.</div>

>> <div class="note">The Tao-tsang edition reads: "then the subjects deceive their prince".</div>

Then there is no faith.

> <div class="commentary">If the prince's faith towards his subjects is not sufficient, then the subjects correspond to this and cheat their prince through faithlessness.</div>

Cautious, o! are these precious words!

> <div class="commentary">This wants to say: The princes of highest antiquity, whenever they had to undertake something, were cautious and rated it high to put stress on their words. They were afraid to detach themselves from Tao and to lose naturalness.</div>

Merit is acquired, and the deed follows.

> <div class="commentary">This is called the great peace of the world.</div>

All the people say: we are natural.

> <div class="commentary">The people do not know the fulness of their prince's Te. On the contrary, they think it but natural.</div>
