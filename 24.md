# Ch. 24 How to embitter kindness.

Who advances has no firm stand.

> <div class="commentary">跂 = 進 to advance. This means: Who covets power, loves glory and advances towards merit and fame, cannot for long give a firm stand to the body and walk in Tao.</div>

Who straddles does not walk.

> <div class="commentary">Who glorifies himself and enlarges himself before others, everybody will block up his way so that he cannot walk.</div>

Who regards himself is not enlightened.

> <div class="commentary">If somebody regards his own appearance, he thinks well of it. If somebody regards his own behaviour, he thinks it conforming to Tao. But he is ignorant of the vulgarity of his appearance and of his behaviour.</div>

Who exists for himself is not clear.

> <div class="commentary">Who thinks of himself as positive and of others as negative, is impeded by all the others and thereby unable to reach clearness.</div>

>> <div class="note">Instead of "all the others" v.1. "all other men" (Che-yao).</div>

Who praises himself accomplishes nothing.

> <div class="commentary">Who inconsiderately boasts of his own merit as being perfect, loses his merit in regard to others.</div>

Who boasts of himself is not enduring.

> <div class="commentary">Who likes to vaunt and to magnify himself, is unable to endure long.</div>

This in its relation to Tao is called greediness for extra food.

> <div class="commentary">贅 = 貪 to be greedy. If such a man who praises and vaunts himself is allowed to conduct the government of a country, he will daily impose taxes and be greedy for extra profit and food. Thereby he behaves greedily.</div>

The creatures all hate him.

> <div class="commentary">If such a man sits upon the throne, this incites his desire to be hurtful. Therefore there is no creature that does not dread and hate him.</div>

Therefore who possesses Tao does not stay there.

> <div class="commentary">This means: A man who possesses Tao does not stay in such a country.</div>
